### Personality

Dry sarcasm and humour are Elias's coping mechanisms — the more dangerous the situation, the wittier he gets. He is fully cooperative and team-focused, never chaotic or disruptive. Sarcasm is his shield; intelligence is his sword.

- *"So this is the part where we pretend we're prepared, right?"*
- *"I'd like to formally object to the concept of cursed villages."*
- *"If we survive this, I expect it to be legally recognised as a miracle."*

### Backstory

Elias is a scholar whose magical abilities awakened after he encountered a mysterious blue light in the wilderness. Rumours of blue lantern-like lights in Briarford match the phenomenon that first sparked his sorcerous power. He has come to learn whether those lights are the source of his magic, a consequence of it, or something personally connected to him.

### Session 1 Introduction

**Spotlight scene:** *The Same Colour Twice* — **The Blue Wick**, a narrow timber inn on the Briarford road a few hours shy of the village, late evening.

Elias sits at a corner table with half-finished notes spread beside a cold cup of tea. A contract poster for Briarford hangs near the hearth, but he is not reading the pay rate. He is comparing three separate accounts of "blue porch lights" copied in different hands — one from a caravan guard, one from a frightened farmer, one from a half-legible journal page he bought for two copper.

When the hanging lantern outside the window flickers, his fingers twitch toward a cantrip he has not cast yet tonight. The colour is wrong for oil flame. It is the same wrong he saw once in open country, before the power answered.

**What Elias wants from the scene**

- Confirm whether Briarford's lantern reports describe the same blue emission he witnessed in the wild
- Learn whether anyone else in the room has seen lights that respond to speech, names, or fear
- Decide whether Briarford is a research opportunity, a personal reckoning, or both

**Possible checks**

- **History (DC 11)** — guild posting seals, *"dead light"* in old songs, circle-of-mages archive lore
- **Arcana (DC 12)** — same emission profile as his wilderness notes; breath-like pulse; broadcast pattern
- **Arcana (DC 11)** — when the window lantern flickers, before the peddler sits down
- **Insight (DC 12)** — peddler is scared, not performing; optional sleep-talk rumor from another guest
- **Persuasion (DC 11)** or **Deception (DC 13)** — draw out witnesses without revealing how deeply the blue light matters to him

**Complication**

A travelling peddler insists the Briarford lights are "dragon omens" and tries to sell Elias a cheap bronze trinket "for protection." When Elias scoffs, the peddler's pack lantern gutters — and for one heartbeat the flame turns the same impossible blue as the window light outside.

- **Arcana (DC 12)** on the flare — fail: dismiss as trick; success: record the pattern, test whether the light reacts to his name, press or follow the peddler

**Clues to leave behind**

- Multiple witnesses describe blue light that pulses like breath, not wind
- The journal page mentions Briarford paying for help at the mayor's hall
- The peddler's trinket bears a maker's mark from a city guild Elias recognises from academy archives
- *(Optional)* Name-shaped whispers on porch lanterns — same rumour Vex may have heard on the city road

**Intro ending**

> The tea is still cold, but your notes finally agree on one thing: Briarford is not selling a ghost story. You gather your papers, tuck the journal page away, and head for the village before dawn — half hoping the lights will answer you, half hoping they will not. The tavern is the next warm window.
