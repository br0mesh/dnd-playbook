**Player Name:** ____________________

**Class:** Ranger
**Level:** 1
**Background:** Outlander
**Alignment:** Unaligned

### Personality

An inquisitive, kind wanderer — always ready to help, though her curiosity and love of animals sometimes lead to comic mishaps.

- *"He's not stealing. He's relocating dinner to a safer location. Under that bush. Where I can't reach."*
- *"I only need enough coin for a house in the woods and a shelter for five hundred cats."*
- *"My friends say after the tenth cat you stop noticing the difference. I think they're mathematically illiterate."*

### Backstory

Ruta has walked forest roads for seasons, trading kindness for campfire stories and odd jobs. Travellers near Briarford spoke of porch lanterns burning the wrong colour — blue, steady, unafraid of wind. She filed the rumour beside her real ambition: earn enough to buy a cottage in the trees and open a sanctuary for five hundred cats. (Her friends insist the headcount is excessive. She insists they have never tried.)

She is not hired yet. She is close enough to look.

### Session 1 Introduction

**Spotlight scene:** *The Cat and the Blue Light* — a mossy clearing off the Briarford forest road, early evening.

Ruta crouches at the edge of a cook-fire ring, tin plate in one hand, dried fish gone. A **ginger tom** — bold, orange, unrepentant — has dragged her supper into a tangle of bracken twenty feet downslope. She could let it go. She will not let it go. The cat is clearly a menace. She is clearly responsible for its welfare now.

While she negotiates with thorns and feline contempt, a **blue glow** threads through the pines toward the village — not firefly, not moon, not any lantern oil she knows. The cat freezes. So does she.

**What Ruta wants from the scene**

- Recover her dinner without harming the cat (ideally befriend it)
- Learn whether the blue light is village trouble, forest witchery, or something worth coin
- Decide whether Briarford is a paying job, a detour, or both

**Possible checks**

- **Animal Handling (DC 11)** — coax the ginger tom out of the bracken with fish scrap, string, or shameless baby-talk
- **Survival (DC 12)** — read tracks: the cat came from the village road; something else crossed the clearing minutes ago wearing city boots
- **Perception (DC 12)** — count blue lights through the trees; spot a contract notice nailed to a trail marker on the downslope path
- **Stealth (DC 11)** — circle the bracken without spooking the cat into deeper brush

**Complication**

The blue glow **brightens once** — slow, like a breath — and the ginger tom **yowls** at the empty air, not at Ruta. For a heartbeat the bracken shadows look too deep, as if the light were looking back.

- On a failed **Animal Handling**, the cat bolts **toward the village lights** — Ruta must choose chase, abandon dinner, or follow the blue glow on her own
- On success, the cat stays — but **Perception (DC 13)** may still catch a second blue pulse out of sync deeper in the trees (same phenomenon Lilith and Mordain may report from other angles)

**Clues to leave behind**

- At least one porch lantern toward Briarford burns **blue** while others hold ordinary flame
- A **contract notice** on a trail marker: Mayor Alden hires for unnatural lantern behavior
- The ginger tom came from the **village road**; fresh **city-boot prints** crossed the clearing before dusk
- *(Optional)* The blue pulse makes animals nervous before humans notice anything wrong

**Intro ending**

> The cat may or may not be in your arms. Your dinner is definitely a loss. But the blue light keeps breathing through the pines toward Briarford, and someone there is already paying for answers. You shoulder your bow, check your string coil, and take the downslope path — one more curious soul headed toward the wrong-coloured lanterns.
