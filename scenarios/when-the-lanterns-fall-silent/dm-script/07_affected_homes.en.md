# Affected Homes: Real Lantern Evidence

**Type:** Investigation · **Location:** Forest-facing lane — **Widow Coyle's cottage** and **the Fenwick porch** · **Pacing:** ~20–25 minutes

### Summary

**Purpose:** Prove the **true** lantern phenomenon with physical evidence before or alongside the fake-lantern subplot. Let PCs **compare intro observations** to real porches — pulse, whispers, tracks toward the **old forest trail**.

**Ends when:** The party has examined at least **one** home in depth (both if time allows), collected **forest-road traces**, and spotted the **volunteer initials** on the lane post — then moves to the next chosen node or back toward the hall.

**Homes:**

| Home | Occupant | State |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Widow Coyle's cottage** | **Widow Coyle** — missing three nights | Door **ajar**, hearth **cold**, porch lantern bracket empty but **blue ash** on the step |
| **The Fenwick porch** | **Fenwick Cropp** lives here (hall is his day job) — neighbor **Tessa Aldridge** saw the second flicker | Porch lantern **still hung** — burns yellow by day; scorch ring shows recent **blue** burn; boot scuffs toward trail |

### Read-aloud

**Approach**

> The forest-facing lane narrows where gardens give up to bracken. Morning birds go quiet when the wind shifts from the trees.
>
> **Widow Coyle's cottage** sits closest to the old trail — door standing open a hand's width, wash line empty, porridge pot cold on the stoop. Where a porch lantern should hang, only a blackened hook and a smear of **blue-grey ash** like crushed glow-moss — but wrong colour, wrong grain.
>
> Two houses down, **the Fenwick porch** — map-writer Fenwick's own front step — still bears a lantern in its bracket. In daylight it looks ordinary yellow. Up close, the glass bears a **second scorch ring** inside the first, as if something burned **through** the flame.

**Optional second lantern flicker (if Lilith, Ruta, or Mordain saw out-of-sync lights)**

> While you argue over the ash, **Tessa Aldridge** two doors down gasps — her porch light hiccups **yellow to blue and back**, a beat **out of sync** with the Fenwick porch. Then holds yellow again.

### Checks

**Investigation (DC 12)** — Widow Coyle's cottage interior

| Result | Find |
|--------|------|
| **Fail** | Messy but mundane — no struggle. |
| **Success** | **No forced entry** — chair pushed back as if someone stood mid-meal; **lantern wick trimmed** on the table; mud only on **threshold**, not boot-scuffed inside. |
| **Success by 5+** | Mud tracks **exit** toward forest road — **light tread**, not dragged. Chalk on inner doorframe: *"Ask the mayor"* (same beat as Lilith Option 4). |

**Investigation (DC 11)** — Fenwick porch lantern hardware

| Result | Find |
|--------|------|
| **Fail** | Ordinary oil lamp. |
| **Success** | **Soot pattern** wrong for wind — flare burst **upward**, not sideways. Oil smells normal; ash does not. |
| **Success by 5+** | Tiny **name-shaped scratch** on the bracket screw — as if someone heard a whisper and tightened grip without knowing. |

**Arcana (DC 12)** — Impossible flame behavior (either porch)

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Fail** | "Bad wick, bad luck." |
| **Success** | Blue phase is **not** evocation cantrip work — no spell residue like `light_cantrip_evocation`. Pulse feels **answered** from the treeline, not lit locally. |
| **Success by 5+** | When forest wind rises, porch glass **dims one beat before** a blue flicker — **breath rhythm** (**Elias** intro). |

**Perception (DC 12)** — Lane and trail mouth

**Insight (DC 10)** — Tessa Aldridge (nervous neighbor)

| Result | Detail |

- Fake lanterns (**08**) may copy this behaviour — do not conclude theft explains Coyle.
