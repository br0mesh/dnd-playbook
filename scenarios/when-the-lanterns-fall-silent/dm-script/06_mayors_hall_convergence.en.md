# Mayor's Hall Convergence: First Light Briefing

**Type:** Group scene · **Location:** Mayor Alden's hall · **Pacing:** ~25–35 minutes · **Teaches:** Social investigation, Insight on evasion, choosing investigation nodes

### Summary

**Purpose:** Bring the full party together for the first time, introduce **Mayor Alden** and **Fenwick Cropp** (clerk and map writer), establish payment terms, turn solo intro clues into a shared picture, and offer **2–3 investigation options** for the village loop.

**Ends when:** The party chooses where to investigate first — **affected homes** (`07_affected_homes`), **Silas Thornwick's house** (`09_herbalist_house`), **fake-lantern suspects** (`08_fake_lantern_house`), and/or the **mill** (`10_mill_rat_problem`, optional) — and leaves the hall with Alden's reluctant blessing.

**Setting:** First light. **Mayor Alden's hall** is a timber-and-stone house with a public room — bench, cold hearth, notice board, clerk's table stacked with partial maps. Contract copied on the **door outside** (same text as `00_session_framing`). Chickens and woodsmoke outside; tired faces inside.

**Payment terms (Alden states plainly):**

| Term | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Up front** | **5 gp per hireling** when the party **commits to enter the forest** with a credible lead (usually after `11_mayor_followup`). |
| **On resolution** | **25 gp split** among the party if Briarford's lantern crisis is **ended to Alden's satisfaction** — vague on purpose. |
| **Room and board** | **The Hearth & Horn** extends credit while they investigate; no luxury. |
| **Records** | City guild **posting fee** paid — same stamp family as intro contracts. |

**Facts Alden admits (before pressure):**

- Porch lanterns flicker **blue** near the **old forest trail**.
- **Three villagers** missing this fortnight — he says **two** until caught in a lie.
- **Silas Thornwick** (herbalist) and **Mara Kell** (hunter) both vanished after asking about the lights.
- A **volunteer group** took the same poster job **eight days ago** — no word since.

**Volunteer trail (Scene 7 layer — visible on arrival):**

- Rolled **bedroll** under the bench — initials **J.R.** on the strap; unpaid **contract chit** tucked inside (same poster text, no guild stamp on the copy).
- **Fenwick Cropp** mutters they slept here two nights and left at dawn asking about the herbalist.

### Read-aloud

**Arrival**

> First grey light. Briarford smells of wet thatch and last night's bread. On the hall door, the **WANTED — BRAVE HANDS** notice you have seen on every road marker — same guild corner stamp, same promise of fair coin.
>
> Inside, a broad-shouldered man in a mayor's chain that sits too heavy on him — **Mayor Alden** — pours tea that has already gone cold. At a side table, **Fenwick Cropp** spreads an ink-stained regional map; his fingers leave smudges on the forest edge. Under the public bench, a **bedroll** nobody has claimed.
>
> Alden looks up as if counting heads. "You all answered the same poster. Good. I need you to look at lanterns, not gossip. And I need you back."

**If Ruta befriended the ginger tom**

> A ginger tom sits on the hall **steps**, washing a paw as if he owns the appointment. He bonks **Ruta's** boot once — comic beat, no roll.

**Alden's pitch**

> "Unnatural light on porches. People walking toward the trees. I have paid for posting in **Ashmere** and on the forest road because Briarford cannot afford silence anymore. Two — **two** — good folk missing that I will name. The rest is rumor until you prove it."
>
> He avoids eye contact on the word *missing*.

**Rumors in the waiting room (seed any 2–3)**

### Checks

**Insight (DC 12)** — Is Alden minimizing the crisis?

| Result | What the party reads |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Fail (< 12)** | He sounds tired but honest — two missing, maybe three. |
| **Success (12+)** | He **counts wrong on purpose** — glance at Fenwick when volunteers are mentioned; jaw tight on "two." |
| **Success by 5+ (17+)** | He **fears riots** more than necromancy — hiding **scale**, not cause. |

**Passive Insight 12:** Treat as success.

#### Branch A — Alden not pressed

Alden points to **Widow Coyle's cottage** on the forest lane ("first blue porch anyone remembers") and says Silas's house is **unlocked but untouched** — "Search if you must. Don't steal herbs."

- Volunteer detail stays on the bedroll only until players examine it.
- Fenwick offers a **partial map** — village, forest edge, old trail dashed, mines marked "?"

#### Branch B — Party presses (Persuasion or Intimidation DC 13)

**Option 1 — Persuasion**

- **Success:** Alden admits **five** missing if you include the volunteers and Mara Kell. "I did not want a panic."
- **Failure:** He repeats two names — **Silas** and **Wren Holloway** — and shuts down.

**Option 2 — Intimidation**

- **Success:** Same as Persuasion success; Alden flustered, not hostile.
- **Failure:** Fenwick steps between you — "He is afraid, not lying about the lights." No extra names tonight.

**On any Branch B success, Alden adds:**

> "The **volunteers** asked about Silas **and** Mara before they left. Stablehand saw them on the herbalist lane at dusk."

**Investigation (DC 12)** — Notice board and Fenwick's records

| Result | Find |
|--------|------|
| **Fail** | Duplicate posters, grain tallies, nothing new. |
| **Success** | **Dates** beside missing names — three disappearances **after** the volunteers left. |
| **Success by 5+** | Guild **posting fee receipt** matches intro stamps; marginal note in Fenwick's hand: *"Dead light sees the road — old shrine verse?"* (**Mordain** intro) |

**History (DC 11)** — Fenwick's shrine note

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Success** | Local verse about lanterns going quiet before bad winters — folklore, not proof. |
| **Fail** | Fenwick shrugs — "My grandmother sang it. I draw maps, not prophecies." |

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Volunteer group **missing** — bedroll, chit, stablehand rumor (after pressure).
- **Silas** and **Mara** both followed lantern leads.
- First affected lantern near **forest-facing road**.
- Alden hides **scale**, not the phenomenon itself.
- Fenwick has **partial map** — full route after they return with forest proof.

**Convergence hooks back to intros:**

- **Name whispers** — rumor + optional Lilith sketch → physical traces in `07`.
- **Breath-pulse / pulse notes** — Elias + Lilith → compare at affected porches.
- **Divine Sense / desecration** — Mordain → Religion check at real lantern in `07`.
- **Animal reaction** — Ruta → Perception advantage near affected homes.
- **Guild stamp / ledger thread** — Vex → Investigation receipt ties city money to Briarford hire.

### Contingencies

- **If players skip sharing intro clues:** NPC rumors cover the same beats — do not block progress.
- **If they attack Alden:** Fenwick calls for neighbors; hire cancelled until de-escalation. Point to investigation, not murder hobbits.
- **If they fixate on volunteers:** Bedroll + Branch B pressure is enough for session 1 — bodies wait for session 2 mines.
- **If they want every node:** Warn time — pick **two or three** before `11_mayor_followup`.
- **If Vex hides Harry Potter:** Fine — but contract recognition on the door still lands.
- **If party splits:** Run fastest scene first; reconvene at herbalist or hall.

### DM Notes

- **Pacing:** 25–35 minutes — briefing, one social round, one investigation pass, choose nodes.
- **Mandatory:** Yes — first group scene of session 1.
- **Fenwick Cropp:** Ink-stained clerk; **not** a linguist; terrified of old shrine verses; session 5 city hook. Name him consistently — he writes the map, he does not solve the woods.
- **Truth to hide:** No "bound souls" reveal. Undead sense, whispers, and blue light are **symptoms**.
- **Cat:** Flavour only — reappears on hall steps if Ruta succeeded on Animal Handling.
- **Next scenes:** Player-chosen nodes `07`–`10`, then `11_mayor_followup`, then `12_forest_edge_closing`.
