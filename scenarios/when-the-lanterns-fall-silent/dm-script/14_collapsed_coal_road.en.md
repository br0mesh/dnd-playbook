# Collapsed Coal Road: Herbs and Ash-Eyed Wolves

**Type:** Travel · navigation · optional combat · **Pacing:** ~25–35 minutes

### Summary

**Purpose:** Follow Fenwick's sketch and Mara's bearing along the **disused coal forest road** — collapsed, overgrown, no longer maintained since Briarford ore ships to **Kettlebrook**. Gather **useful forest herbs**, survive an **ash-eyed wolf pack**, and reach the mine hill without a city-road detour.

**Ends when:** Party arrives at the **Briarford coal mines** approach (`16_coal_mine_arrival`) — roughly 2–3 hours in-fiction from `13`.

**Setting:** Old **coal forest road** branching north from the old forest trail — washouts, fallen timber, rusted cart wheels half-buried in briar.

### Read-aloud

**Road mouth**

> The trail forks where an older **coal road** cuts into the pines — or what is left of it. Briarford hasn't sent wagons this way in years; **Kettlebrook** takes the deep shafts now. Ruts end in a **washout**: mud, splintered ties, a warning sign face-down in the moss. Wheel tracks don't continue — but **boot prints** and **small clawed feet** do, picking a path along the high bank.

**Mara Kell note (if not given in Session 1)**

> Tacked under a split rail, waterproof oilcloth — Mara's blocky hand:

> *Tracks behind the mill are wrong. Too many small feet. Coal mine first. Shrine after, if I still have daylight.*

### Checks

**Survival (DC 12)** — Find a passable route along the collapsed road

| Result | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| **Fail** | Lose **30 minutes**; detour through thicket — still arrive at mine |
| **Success** | Steady pace; spot herb patches below |
| **Success by 5+** | Shortcut along the bank — wolves surprised (party acts first) |

**Investigation (DC 11)** — Read whose prints share the road

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Success** | Human boots (**Mara's** size) overlap **smaller clawed prints** — goblin-sized, recent |
| **Success by 5+** | Scrap of oiled goblin leather snagged on briar |

#### Branch A — Herb gathering (optional, encouraged if Lilith or Silas threads)

**Nature or Survival (DC 11)** — Identify useful growth along the bank

| Result | Find |
|--------|------|
| **Fail** | Bitter weeds only |
| **Success** | **Yarrow** — stops bleeding; one use stabilizes a dying ally or grants **1d4 HP** once outside combat (story reagent, not an item card) |
| **Success by 5+** | Also **bittercap moss** — antidote prep; echoes Silas's notes; useful at Session 3 shrine if carried |

**Arcana (DC 12)** — Lilith callback on herb samples

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Success** | Leaves **shiver** near old coal dust — ambient wrongness, not a curse on the plants |

#### Branch B — Ash-eyed wolf pack

**Perception (DC 12)** — Before combat

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Success** | **Three wolves** downwind — eyes **ash-grey** with faint ember glow, same wrongness as Garrick's mill rats |
| **Fail** | Wolves close from the treeline when the party spreads on the bank |

**Combat — wolf encounter**

Use monster sheet **`05_ash_eyed_wolf_monster`** — default **3** for 4–5 PCs; **2** for 3 PCs.

| Teaching beat | DM tip |
|---------------|--------|
| **Pack Tactics** | Two wolves on one target — call out advantage once |
| **Prone rider** | Bite knockdown — one demonstration |
| **Light / torches** | Wolves hesitate at bright fire — Intimidation or Animal Handling escape hatch |

**Read-aloud when wolves circle:**

> Three wolves pace the high bank — coats matted with coal dust, eyes catching light like **cold embers**. They are not rabid. They are **wrong** the way the mill rats were wrong — touched by something nearby, not born of it.

**After wolves:**

> The pack breaks north or dies quiet. The coal-smell strengthens. Through the trees, a black scar on the hillside — the **Briarford coal mines**.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Road **abandoned** — Briarford is a backwater pit mouth, not the hub.
- Goblins use this route — prints confirmed.
- Herbs for later — yarrow / bittercap if gathered.
- Wolves = **ambient contact** with regional wrongness — not necromancer pets.

### Contingencies

- **If party tries main Ashmere road instead:** One soft gate — Survival DC 12: fresh **Kettlebrook** wagon mud east, not mine smoke; loop back or lose an hour.
- **If wolves TPK risk:** Wolves flee at two down; yarrow allows post-fight recovery.
- **If herbs skipped:** No penalty — mine cabin and shrine still provide resources later.
- **If Ruta + ginger tom:** Cat arches back — early warning grants advantage on first Perception for wolves.

### DM Notes

- **Pacing:** 25–35 minutes — navigation + one fight OR social scare-off + herbs.
- **Mandatory:** Yes — only overland route to mine in this scenario.
- **Truth to hide:** Ash eyes = **edge effect** of soul-lantern field — do not name necromancer.
- **Kettlebrook:** Bigger coal village east; Briarford mine is **legacy**, mostly sealed — explains collapse.
