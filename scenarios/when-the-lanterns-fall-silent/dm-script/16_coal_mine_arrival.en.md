# Coal Mine Arrival: Blockade, Volunteers, and Blasting Powder

**Type:** Investigation · problem-solving · optional loot · **Pacing:** ~30–40 minutes

### Summary

**Purpose:** Deliver the mine set-piece **before** the crawl — **main entrance blockaded**, goblin-sized breach too narrow for humans, **volunteer bodies** with lootable gear, **miner storage cabin** with lockable door and **blasting charges** to reopen the shaft.

**Ends when:** Party **breaches the main entrance** (explosives or clever alternative) and is ready to enter `17_mine_crawl` — noise may alert goblins inside.

**Setting:** **Briarford coal mines** — hillside cut, timber props, abandoned cart track. Small **miner storage cabin** on a stone pad near the mouth.

### Read-aloud

**First sight**

> The trees thin around a **black wound** in the hillside — Briarford's old pit, long eclipsed by **Kettlebrook**'s deeper seams. The **main entrance** is **choked**: timbers wedged crosswise, coal baskets, fallen rock — deliberate blockage, not natural cave-in. Coal dust hangs in the air. Somewhere inside, metal **clinks**.

**Secondary opening**

> Along the collapse, a **narrow gap** — knee-high, shoulder-wide at best. Clawed prints go in and out. A goblin could wriggle; a human in armor would not.

### Checks

**Investigation (DC 10)** — Read the blockade

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Success** | Blockade is **recent** — axe marks on props, not mine-age rot |
| **Success by 5+** | Goblins **want** humans kept out while they hold the tunnels |

**Perception (DC 12)** — Sounds from the hill

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Success** | Chittering echo — **goblins** inside, not trapped miners at the mouth |
| **Fail** | Wind in the gap — could be anything |

#### Branch A — Volunteer remains (Scene 7 payoff)

**Placement:** Off the track near the cabin — **checking the surroundings**, not on the main path.

**Read-aloud:**

> Three shapes behind a fallen cart — wrong angles, wrong stillness. Bedroll straps, guild-stamped chits, Briarford clay on their boots. **Rusty swords** and **holed leather armor** — pierced clean through, not torn by teeth.

**Volunteer identities (match Session 1 initials):**

| Initials | Name | Gear tell |
|----------|------|-----------|

| **S.M.** | **Sera Marsh** | Mercenary patch; sword rusted at the guard |

**Investigation (DC 11)** — Cause of death

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Success** | Wounds from **blades and spears** — **humanoid**, not wolves or claws |
| **Success by 5+** | They died **facing** the blockade — killed guarding the approach, not fleeing |

**Loot (allow — gear is poor):**

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Rusty shortswords (×2)** | Treat as **shortswords** with disadvantage on first attack each combat until cleaned (flavor) — or plain **1d6** improvised |
| **Holed leather armor (×2)** | **AC 11** if worn — each hit that deals damage has **25% chance** to worsen hole (no extra AC loss; flavor only) |
| **Contract chits, 4 sp total**, rope (50 ft., frayed), **1 torch** | Practical salvage |
| **Jonas's journal scrap** | *"Something small blocked the big door. We tried the cabin."* |

**Medicine (DC 10):** Dead **2–4 days** — before the party took the job.

#### Branch B — Miner storage cabin

**Read-aloud:**

> A squat **timber cabin** on a stone pad — **miners' storage**, by the rusted tool marks and coal-oil stains. Sturdy door, iron **lock**, heavy hinges. Window shutters bolted from inside. A painted board, faded: *Briarford Colliery — Store*.

**Strength (DC 18)** — Force the door

| Result | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| **Fail** | Door **does not give** — reinforced frame; forcing wastes time and noise |
| **Success** | Still **not recommended** — DM may allow but lock survives bent |

**Thieves' tools (DC 13)** — Pick the lock

| Result | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| **Fail** | Pick snaps; retry allowed; or try window shutters Investigation DC 12 |
| **Success** | Door opens — dry goods, tools, **blasting supplies** |

**Investigation (DC 11)** — Shutter latch (alternative entry)

| Result | Outcome |
|--------|---------|
| **Success** | Rear shutter weak — same interior access without breaking the main lock |

**Inside — read-aloud:**

> Shelves of picks, lamp oil, spare rope, nail kegs. In the back, **wooden crates** stencilled with a flame symbol — **mining charges**: powder, packed stone, fuse cord. Each crate is **too heavy to haul far** — forty pounds or more — but one **prepared charge** could open the blocked entrance.

**Blasting powder (problem, not loot card):**

| Detail | Rule |
|--------|------|
| **Weight** | Whole crates cannot be taken on the road — **one charge** prepared for local use |
| **Setup** | **Investigation DC 10** or miner background — pack charge against blockade timbers |
| **Fuse** | **1 round** fuse default; retreat 30 ft.; loud **bang** |
| **Effect** | Main entrance **partially cleared** — human-sized opening; splinters and dust; **goblins inside alerted** |
| **Failure** | Failed setup DC — fizzle or weak blow; retry with second charge if time; only **two** charges practical |

**Arcana or Perception (DC 12)** — Old mining lantern on a shelf

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Success** | Hood scratched with **coal mine** mark; if lit, faint **blue edge** before normal flame — matches mill reward in `10` |

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Volunteers killed by **humanoids** — goblins hold the mine.
- Cabin confirms mine was **active storage** until Kettlebrook shift.
- Explosives = **key tool** — teach problem-solving before crawl combat.
- Noise from blast = goblins **ready** in `17` — fair trade for human-sized entry.

### Contingencies

- **If party skips looting volunteers:** Blockade problem unchanged — journal scrap optional in cabin Investigation DC 10.
- **If no rogue / no thieves' tools:** Shutter route, or **Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) DC 15** improvising picks; dwarf/gnome may squeeze **gap** with armor off — **Athletics DC 14**, single-file, no shield — risky alternate.
- **If they refuse explosives:** Long **Athletics DC 16** labour clearing one timber layer — **1 hour**, same alert noise on final crash.
- **If Session 1 skipped volunteer clues:** Initials on gear still match `06`/`07` when named.
- **Mara Kell:** Boot prints past volunteers toward gap — still **missing**; chalk in `17`.
