# Intro: Vex — The Wrong Name

**Character:** Vex (tiefling rogue) · **Player spotlight:** ~5–10 minutes · **Teaches:** Insight, optional unarmed strike, Stealth exit

### Summary

On the Briarford road, a league outside the city walls, Vex rests at **The Last Lamp** — a squat timber-and-stone tavern on the forest side of the road, where wagon ruts turn to mud and city smoke still stains the horizon behind you. Late afternoon; rain has stopped but the eaves still drip. Sawdust, wet wool, cheap ale. Caravan guards dice in a corner. A clerk from the city assayer's office eats alone, ink on his cuffs.

Vex sits in a corner booth, back to the wall, hood over his horns. A **contract notice** for Briarford lies on the table. He is reading the pay line and listening to the room — not drinking heavily, not bothering anyone.

Someone at the bar speaks his **real name** and mutters about his old crew (fate unknown). An **Insight** check decides whether Vex misreads the moment as a bounty hunter closing in, or keeps his head long enough to choose a smarter exit toward the village on the poster.

**Ends when:** Vex leaves the tavern (and usually the city road) headed for **Briarford** — contract in pocket, at least one unanswered question about who spoke his name, and **Harry Potter** as the false name he will use in the village.

**Contract poster (handout or paraphrase):**

> **WANTED — BRAVE HANDS**
>
> The village of **Briarford** seeks aid with **unnatural lantern behavior** and **recent disappearances** near homes where porch lights act strangely.
>
> Fair payment upon investigation and resolution.
>
> **Speak with Mayor Alden** at the village hall.
>
> *(City guild stamp in the corner — posting fee paid.)*

### Read-aloud

**Arrival**

> The Last Lamp smells of wet ash and fried onions. Outside, the city walls are a grey smudge two miles back; ahead, the Briarford road cuts through darkening pines. You have not given anyone your real name in three days — hood up, coin down, gone before questions stick.
>
> On the table, a folded notice from the village hall promises **fair coin** for anyone who can explain lanterns that burn the wrong colour and people who walk toward the trees and do not come home. Mayor Alden's name is at the bottom. The work is vague. The money is not.
>
> For now, nobody is bothering you. That is about to change.

**The name at the bar**

After a minute of quiet, a voice at the **bar** cuts through the dice game:

> "...no, I'm telling you — **Vex** was in on the ledger job. Whole crew went sideways after that. Some say the **Ashmere lot** got them. Some say they ran. I haven't seen a soul from that string since winter."

The speaker is **Tomas Grull** — broad-shouldered, one milky eye, city boots dusty from forest roads. He is **not** looking at Vex yet. He is arguing with the barkeep and a tired caravan guard, half drunk, half boasting.

Then Tomas's good eye **flicks to your corner** — too long to be accidental. He goes quiet. The guard follows his gaze. The room does not explode; it **tightens**. Someone near the door shifts weight.

**Vex knows:** "Ashmere lot" was your old string's name in Thieves' Cant. You do not know what happened to them after the ledger theft. Nobody has said your real name aloud in months.

**Panic branch (failed Insight)**

> The name hangs in the air like a noose. Your chest says *run*. The man at the bar is already standing. You do not wait to see whether he pulls steel or paper.

**Flight ending**

> You leave the Last Lamp at a run where the road turns. Mud grabs at your boots. Behind you, someone shouts **Vex** once — not triumph, something closer to panic — and then the pines swallow it. The contract creases in your fist. Briarford is hours ahead. You already know what name they will get there: **Harry Potter**. Forward feels safer than back.

**Talk outside (success branch, Option 3)**

> "Gods — you *are* alive." Tomas's voice cracks. "I heard they took the whole string except runners. I didn't know if you got clear."

### Checks

**Insight (DC 12)** — Who is speaking, and what do they want?

| Result | What Vex believes | What is actually true (DM only) |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Fail (< 12)** | Tomas is a **bounty hunter** who has identified you and is stalling while allies move. | Tomas is a **former runner** from the Ashmere string — scared, drunk, and **testing** whether you are still alive. He has no warrant and no backup. |
| **Success (12+)** | You read the pause correctly: he recognized you, but he is **more frightened than hunting**. | Same truth — he wants to know if you survived, and whether you still have what was stolen. |

**Passive Insight 12:** Treat as success.

#### Branch A — Failed Insight (panic)

Vex stands, chair scraping. Tomas's hand goes to his belt — **knife**, not a bounty badge.

**Optional unarmed strike (if Vex attacks first):**

- **Unarmed strike:** +2 to hit (Dex 15 — fist or shove), **1 bludgeoning** damage on hit.
- Tomas has **AC 11**, **7 HP**. On any hit, he **stumbles**; on a miss, he **blocks** and shouts for the barkeep.

**Tavern reaction:** Barkeep **Mira** bangs the counter — "Out. Both of you. I don't care why." Two caravan guards clear a path to the door.

**Exit:**

- **Stealth (DC 11)** — slip out the **back through the kitchen** before Tomas reaches the road. Advantage if you dropped something shiny as distraction (**Sleight of Hand DC 10**).
- **Failure:** Tomas spots you on the road and **does not follow far** — he only wants to talk, but Vex does not know that yet. A thrown dagger (1d4+2) might clip a pack strap; no pursuit past the first bend.

**Where Vex goes:** **Briarford.** Poster in pocket or pack. The village is work, coin, and a door before anyone learns your real name.

#### Branch B — Success (choose your exit)

You have a beat to act before the room decides what you are.

**Option 1 — Bluff**

- **Deception (DC 11)** — "Wrong tiefling. My name is **Harry Potter**."
- **Success:** Tomas wavers; the guard loses interest. Finish your drink, pay, leave at a **normal pace**. Tomas may follow at a distance (**Stealth opposed by his Perception +1**).
- **Failure:** Tomas calls the bluff softly: "Don't. Not here." Barkeep watches. You can still leave, but **without surprise**.
- **Either way:** **Harry Potter** is the name for **Briarford** — mayor, tavern, other PCs. Only Tomas connects you to **Vex**.

**Option 2 — Slip away**

- **Stealth (DC 12)** — move through the crowd toward the **side door** marked DELIVERIES.
- **Success:** On the muddy track behind the tavern before Tomas reaches your empty bench.
- **Failure:** Pot-boy sees you; Tomas runs out the front. Let him catch you outside (talk) or **sprint** for the tree line (no roll; he won't keep up).

**Option 3 — Follow outside**

No roll to leave — gesture Tomas to the **rain barrel side** of the building.

**What Tomas knows (share 1–2 clues):**

- After the **ledger job**, the Ashmere lot **vanished over a fortnight** — no bodies, no trials, no word.
- He heard **city coin** paid for silence — names on sealed records, same kind of work Briarford's mayor is too scared to put on a poster.
- He is **not** here to collect a bounty; he is **running east** and thought he saw a ghost.

- **Insight (DC 10)** on Tomas: telling the truth as he knows it. He will **not** enter Briarford — "Villages talk."

**Option 4 — Rob**

- **Sleight of Hand (DC 13)** — lift Tomas's belt pouch or **folded chit** in his boot.
- **Success:** **8 sp**, **guild chit** (assayer's mark), scrap: *"...ledger copies — Briarford toll-house?"*
- **Failure:** Tomas catches your wrist; **Athletics contest** or abandon and revert to Bluff / Slip away. Failed contest → barkeep ejects you (Branch A exit, minus fistfight if you talk fast).

**Clues to carry into Briarford (leave with at least two):**

- Briarford pays **fairly**; Mayor Alden hires at the **village hall**.
- A porch lantern **whispered a name** — Tomas heard it from a frightened carter who fled Briarford last week.
- The **Ashmere string** is gone; cause unknown — city money, sealed records, no bodies.
- Tomas (or stolen chit) ties **ledger copies** to the **Briarford toll-road**.
- Speaker wears **city boots** but **forest mud** to the knee.

**Arriving in Briarford (all branches):** Vex introduces himself as **Harry Potter** — village tavern, mayor's hall, other adventurers. Horns under the hood when he can. **Vex** stays buried unless someone from the city road recognizes him.

### Contingencies

- **If Vex never engages:** Tomas eventually leaves without confrontation. Vex can still read the poster and depart for Briarford at dusk — lose the crew clue unless he eavesdrops (**Perception DC 13**).
- **If Vex kills Tomas:** Mira locks the tavern; caravan guards pursue to the tree line. **Murder warrant** flavor in the city — Briarford becomes urgent refuge. Keep combat short; Tomas is not a boss fight.
- **If Vex talks to the clerk:** Assayer's clerk knows nothing about Ashmere — **Perception DC 14** notices he flinches at "sealed records." Part 2 hook only.
- **If the player wants to stay in the city:** Session 1 convergence assumes Vex reaches Briarford before the shared tavern scene. Nudge with rain, gate curfew, or Mira refusing service after a brawl.
- **If Insight fails:** Run Branch A — optional fist attack, then flee toward Briarford as **Harry Potter**.
- **If Insight succeeds:** Player picks Bluff, Slip away, Follow outside, or Rob; all paths end on the road to Briarford.

### DM Notes

- **Spotlight rule:** Address the player directly; other PCs are not present. Solo screen time.
- **Names:** **Vex** is real — old crew and ledger hunters only. **Harry Potter** is the public false name from Briarford onward. Player sheet can show "Harry Potter" as the face he presents.
- **Old crew:** Do **not** reveal Ashmere's fate here. Tomas is wrong, scared, or incomplete.
- **Teaching moment:** Insight = "reading people, not facts" — failure is a **false read**, not bad luck.
- **Fist attack on failure:** One roll only; goal is **escape**, not winning a bar fight at level 1.
- **Next scene:** Vex on the road to Briarford, or arriving at the **Briarford tavern** where the party converges (Mayor Alden).
- **Poster prop:** Pin the same contract text in Briarford later so players recognize it.
