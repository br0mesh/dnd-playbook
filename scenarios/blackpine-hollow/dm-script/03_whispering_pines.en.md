# Scene 3: Whispering Pines

### Summary

Mosswick tests the party with illusion magic in a ring of whispering pines. The scene should feel creepy and personal, but the whispers are not meant to directly harm anyone.

### Read-aloud

> The path opens into a ring of tall pines. Their trunks lean inward like listeners around a secret.
>
> When you step inside the ring, the trees begin to whisper.
>
> At first, it sounds like wind. Then you hear your own voices repeated back to you, but twisted.
>
> "I am not afraid... not afraid... liar..."
>
> "I trust them... trust them... do you?"

### Checks

- **Wisdom save (DC 13):** Resist the fear and keep calm.
- **Arcana (DC 13):** Realize this is illusion magic.
- **Performance or Persuasion (DC 13):** Answer the whispers with confidence and quiet them.

### Contingencies

- **If players attack the trees:** It does not help, but the tree "bleeds" silver sap and laughs.
- **On success:** The whispers fade and one pine trunk splits open just enough to reveal an old symbol carved in the wood: a lantern above a cracked stone door.
- **On failure:** The party still moves forward, but one character has disadvantage on their next Perception or Wisdom check because the whispers linger in their head.

### DM Notes

- Mosswick is testing the party here, not trying to kill them.
- Let the scene spotlight character fears, trust, and bravado.
- Keep the consequences atmospheric and light, not crushing.
