# Scene 8: Endings

### Summary

Resolve the party's choice about Mosswick, then close with a final campfire epilogue. Use the branch that matches what happened in the shrine.

### Read-aloud

#### Branch A — Mosswick Is Sealed

> The cracked stones glow gold. The lantern spirits circle the shrine, brighter and brighter, until the broken door seals with a sound like a deep breath.
>
> Mosswick's grin fades behind the stone. For one moment, he looks less like a monster and more like a lonely child.
>
> "Tell it right," he whispers. "Tell them I was here."
>
> Then the shrine is silent.

#### Branch B — Mosswick Is Freed by Bargain

> Mosswick places one tiny hand over his heart and one over his mouth.
>
> "By root, flame, lantern, and name, I agree."
>
> The lantern spirits flare like stars. The forest exhales. Somewhere far away, a lost traveler remembers their own name.
>
> Mosswick gives you a crooked bow. "I will be good," he says.
>
> Then he grins. "Good-ish."

#### Branch C — Mosswick Escapes

> Mosswick laughs, sharp and bright, and bursts into a cloud of green sparks. The lantern spirits scatter.
>
> For a moment, the forest is silent. Then, from very far away, you hear his voice:
>
> "Next time, bring better snacks!"

#### Final Campfire Epilogue

> By the time you return to camp, the fire is orange again. The tents are still standing. The night sounds have returned: crickets, leaves, distant owls.
>
> One last golden lantern floats above the fire. It dips once, like a thank-you, then rises into the stars.
>
> Each of you notices one small thing changed by the night's adventure.

### Checks

No new checks are required here. Instead, ask each player:
- What changed for your character?

### Contingencies

- **If the players fail badly or choose chaos:** Use Branch C. It is not a total failure; it creates a funny future problem.
- **If the party sealed Mosswick fairly:** Emphasize that the end is sad, not triumphant.
- **If the party bargains successfully:** Let the forest feel relieved, but keep a hint that Mosswick is still mischievous.

### DM Notes

- Endings should reflect tone as much as outcome: bittersweet, strange, funny, or eerie.
- The lantern spirits are witnesses to the party's choice.
- Always end at the campfire, even if the shrine scene was tense or messy.
