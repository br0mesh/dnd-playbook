# Quick DM Reference

### Summary

This appendix collects the fast reference notes from the PDF so you can keep the session moving without flipping around. Use it between scenes, during stalls, or when pacing needs a quick correction.

### Read-aloud

No direct read-aloud is required here. These are facilitator prompts and reference notes for the DM.

### Checks

Use these default difficulty classes when unsure:
- **Easy:** DC 10
- **Medium:** DC 13
- **Hard:** DC 15
- **Very hard:** DC 18

Useful DM phrases:
- "What do you do?"
- "You can try."
- "Yes, and..."
- "Yes, but..."
- "What does that look like?"
- "What are you hoping to learn?"
- "That sounds risky. Are you sure?"
- "I do not know the exact rule, so I will make a quick ruling and we will check later."

### Contingencies

- **If players get stuck:**
  - A lantern spirit moves toward the next location.
  - A character hears a whisper from the shrine.
  - An NPC clue appears as a journal page, symbol, or vision.
  - Mosswick steals something and leaves a trail.
  - Say directly: "You see three obvious options..."
- **If the session runs long:**
  - Skip Moonlit Creek if exploration already feels full.
  - Shorten combat by making enemies flee at half numbers.
  - Move from Abandoned Campsite directly to the Ruined Shrine.
- **If the session is too short:**
  - Add a talking fox who asks for a snack before giving a clue.
  - Let a backpack open itself and rearrange everyone's items.
  - Have the lantern spirits show a memory of Mosswick being sealed.
  - Let the party find a sleeping treasure hunter who caused the problem.

### DM Notes

- Share the spotlight and ask quieter players what they do.
- Do not punish creative ideas; give them a fair roll or a fair cost.
- Keep scary content spooky, not cruel.
- Let players win through talking, tricking, fighting, or problem-solving.
- If everyone is laughing, curious, or pleasantly nervous, the session is working.
