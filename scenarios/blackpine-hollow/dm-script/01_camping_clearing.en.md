# Scene 1: Camping Clearing

### Summary

The camp feels peaceful until the fire turns blue and five lantern spirits appear. This scene should push the party to investigate the lights and step onto the mystery trail.

### Read-aloud

> The sun has disappeared behind the black pine trees. Your tents are up, the fire is warm, and the smell of dinner hangs in the cool night air. For a while, the forest feels peaceful.
>
> Then the normal forest sounds stop.
>
> No crickets. No birds. No wind.
>
> The campfire turns blue. One spark floats upward, but instead of fading, it hangs in the air like a tiny lantern. Then another light appears between the trees. Then another. Five golden lights drift in a line, leading away from camp.
>
> One lantern flickers, almost like it is waiting for you.
>
> What do you do?

### Checks

- **Arcana (DC 13):** The blue fire is fey magic, not normal fire.
- **Nature (DC 12):** The forest silence is unnatural.
- **Perception (DC 13):** There are small old footprints near the tree line.
- **Survival (DC 13):** The lanterns follow an old trail toward deeper forest.

### Contingencies

- **If players stay at camp:** Give them one chance to prepare, then increase pressure: the lanterns drift farther away, the fire dims, and a small voice whispers, "Please... the shrine remembers."
- **If they still refuse:** The mystery comes to them — strange laughter circles the camp, their shadows move incorrectly, or one backpack vanishes and reappears at the tree line.

### DM Notes

- The party should choose to follow the lights by the end of this scene.
- Keep the tone eerie, but beginner-friendly rather than punishing.
- If they need a stronger push, let the lantern spirits visibly wait for them and pulse with urgency.
