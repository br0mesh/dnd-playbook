# Scene 2: Following the Lanterns

### Summary

The lantern spirits lead the party deeper into Blackpine Hollow. Run this as a simple skill challenge: 3 successes before 2 failures, with different players contributing.

### Read-aloud

> The lantern spirits lead you under the branches of Blackpine Hollow. Your campfire disappears behind the trees. The path is narrow, soft with pine needles, and the air smells of wet moss.
>
> Every few steps, one lantern waits until you catch up. Another darts ahead. They seem excited, frightened, or both.

### Checks

Ask for 3 successful checks before 2 failures. Let different players help.

- **Survival (DC 13):** Follow the safe trail.
- **Perception (DC 13):** Notice roots and hidden holes.
- **Nature (DC 13):** Identify dangerous glowing mushrooms.
- **Stealth (DC 13):** Avoid waking something in the brush.
- **Animal Handling (DC 12):** Calm a frightened owl or fox.

### Contingencies

- **If the group gets 3 successes:** They arrive safely and gain a clue or a smoother lead into the next scene.
- **If the group gets 2 failures:** They still arrive, but choose one complication:
  - A character loses 1d4 HP from thorny roots.
  - Someone's boot gets stuck in silver mud.
  - A lantern turns blue and shows a vision of the ruined shrine cracking open.
  - Mosswick steals a harmless item: spoon, button, hat, pipe, or glove.

### DM Notes

- Keep this challenge moving; it should feel like eerie travel, not a puzzle wall.
- Use the lantern spirits as pacing tools: waiting, darting ahead, or flashing blue.
- Success should feel smooth; failure should create trouble, not stop the story.
