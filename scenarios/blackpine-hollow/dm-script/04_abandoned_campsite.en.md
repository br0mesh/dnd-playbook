# Scene 4: Abandoned Campsite

### Summary

The party finds an old campsite full of looping footprints and small clues that point toward Mosswick and the ruined shrine. Give at least two clues even if the rolls are poor.

### Read-aloud

> The lanterns lead you to another campsite. This one is old, cold, and abandoned. A torn bedroll lies beside a circle of black stones. A cracked lantern hangs from a branch, though there is no oil inside it.
>
> On the ground, footprints circle the camp again and again, as if someone walked in loops for hours.

### Checks

- **Investigation (DC 13):** Find the journal page.
- **Survival (DC 13):** Realize the footprints were caused by enchantment.
- **Religion or Arcana (DC 14):** Understand the shrine once sealed fey magic.

### Contingencies

Give at least two clues even on low rolls:
- A journal page: "The green boy asked for my name. I laughed. Now I cannot remember why I came here."
- A broken lantern that matches the spirits' shape but is made of old bronze.
- A shrine symbol: a lantern above a cracked stone door, same as in the pines.
- A small mossy footprint: too small for a human, too sharp for an animal.

Optional funny moment:
- Mosswick returns a stolen item by dropping it into someone's hood, boot, or soup.
- A tiny voice from the trees says, "You dropped this later."

### DM Notes

- This scene is a clue-delivery node, not a dead end.
- The players should leave knowing the shrine matters, the lantern motif is old, and Mosswick has been tampering with travelers.
- If pacing is tight, collapse the clue hunt into two strong details and move on.
