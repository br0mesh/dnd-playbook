# Scene 6: Small Combat

### Summary

After the creek crossing, the lantern spirits scatter and twig blights rise from the ground. This is the tutorial-style combat scene of the one-shot.

### Read-aloud

> As the last of you crosses the creek, the lantern spirits suddenly scatter. The ground cracks with tiny snapping sounds. Dead twigs rise on thin root-legs, twisting into sharp little shapes.
>
> The forest itself seems to whisper: "Turn back."

### Checks

No special checks are required before combat starts. If players need guidance, keep it simple:
- Use the **twig blight** stat block.
- 3 players: 4 twig blights.
- 4 players: 5 twig blights.
- 5 players: 6 twig blights.

### Contingencies

- If the table is new to combat, prompt each turn with: "What do you want to do?"
- If someone hesitates, offer options: attack, cast a spell, move, hide, help someone, use an item, or try something creative.
- Keep enemy turns fast. Put one or two blights on the front line and one on a ranged or spellcasting character.
- When half the enemies are defeated, the rest can flee or collapse into ordinary sticks if pacing feels slow.

### DM Notes

- This is the session's main beginner-combat teaching moment.
- Fire weakness can create a dramatic, satisfying reveal if you want it.
- When the fight ends, the lantern spirits slowly return and guide the party onward.
