# Scene 7: The Ruined Shrine

### Summary

The forest opens onto the ruined shrine, where the party finally meets Mosswick face-to-face. This is the central moral scene of the one-shot: talk, seal, bargain, or fight.

### Read-aloud

> The forest opens into a moonlit hollow. At its center stands a ruined stone shrine, covered in moss and cracked roots. Old lantern hooks hang from broken pillars. Most are empty.
>
> At the shrine door, a circle of golden lantern spirits floats in silence. Inside the circle sits a small green figure with mossy hair, yellow eyes, and a grin much too wide.
>
> He claps slowly.
>
> "Campers! Heroes! Snack-carriers! You made it."

### Checks

**Option A — Repair the Shrine Seal**
- Religion (DC 14)
- Arcana (DC 14)
- Strength (DC 13) to move stones
- Persuasion (DC 15) to convince Mosswick not to resist

**Option B — Bargain with Mosswick**
- Persuasion (DC 14)
- Insight (DC 13)
- Deception (DC 15) if the players try to trick him

**Option C — Fight Mosswick**
- No special setup roll required; go straight into the combat scene using Mosswick's combat entry if the party attacks.

### Contingencies

**What Mosswick says**
- The shrine trapped him long ago.
- The lantern spirits were the shrine's guardians.
- Treasure hunters cracked the seal recently.
- He has been "playing" with travelers because he is angry and lonely.

Use lines like:
- "They locked me under stone and called it peace."
- "I borrowed names, voices, paths. Small things. Mostly."
- "If you seal me again, you are no better than them."
- "If you free me, I might behave. I might even try."

**Possible bargain terms**
- He returns stolen names, voices, and memories.
- He protects travelers in Blackpine Hollow.
- He may play harmless tricks only.
- He must answer when the lantern spirits call.
- The party promises to tell his story truthfully.

### DM Notes

- Mosswick should be unsettling but not purely evil. Let the players talk.
- If the players choose violence, keep the fight cinematic and short.
- If they treat him fairly during sealing, the seal can be softer: he can still speak with the spirits and is not left completely alone.
- This scene should end with a clear choice that feeds directly into `08_endings`.
