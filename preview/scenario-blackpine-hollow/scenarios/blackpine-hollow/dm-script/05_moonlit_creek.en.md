# Scene 5: Moonlit Creek

### Summary

The party reaches a narrow creek with a symbolic crossing puzzle. The intended answer is lantern, leaf, flame, but any clever plan that gets the party across should work.

### Read-aloud

> The trees thin, and moonlight spills across a narrow creek. The water is dark blue and perfectly still, even though it should be flowing. On the far bank, the lantern spirits gather in a nervous cluster.
>
> Half-submerged stones form a path across, but each stone has a different symbol carved into it: flame, leaf, eye, tooth, lantern.

### Checks

- **Investigation (DC 13):** Understand the clue.
- **Athletics (DC 12):** Jump or climb across.
- **Acrobatics (DC 13):** Balance on slippery stones.
- **Nature (DC 13):** Understand the leaf and flame relationship.

### Contingencies

- The clue is: "Light guides the forest, the forest feeds the fire."
- The correct symbolic path is: lantern, leaf, flame.
- Players can also cross by jumping, using rope, freezing water, flying, or any other clever plan.
- **On failure:** No one should die here. A character gets soaked, loses 1d4 HP from cold magical water, or drops one small item that another player can rescue.

### DM Notes

- Treat this as a mood puzzle, not a wall that blocks the session.
- Reward problem-solving over guessing.
- The lantern spirits should clearly want the party to succeed, even if they cannot explain the answer directly.
