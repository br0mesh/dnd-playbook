### Personality

Silent and heavy-footed, like something unfinished. Mordain has wandered without destination since a divine force settled into him uninvited — he does not name it, does not speak of it, and does not trust it yet. He follows where it pulls.

### Backstory

Mordain has no temple, no oath spoken aloud, and no story he offers strangers. Something chose him on the road months ago: warmth behind the ribs, direction without words. Since then he has walked where it tugged — never quickly, never lightly.

Rumors of blue lantern light near Briarford felt like the same signal. Not a choice. A pull. He wants to understand what chose him, and why here, why now.

### Session 1 Introduction

**Spotlight scene:** *Before the Threshold* — the forest edge above Briarford, dusk.

Mordain stands alone beneath the treeline, chain mail hidden under a traveler's cloak, shield strapped to his back. He does not enter yet. From here the village looks ordinary — smoke, rooflines, the last orange of sunset — until a porch lantern on the far side of Briarford flares the wrong colour.

Blue. Steady. Not wind. Not hearth-flame.

The weight in his chest turns toward it like a compass finding north. No one has spoken to him. No contract has been pressed into his hand. He sees the light first, and his body already knows he is going down the hill.

**What Mordain wants from the scene**

- Learn whether the blue lantern answers the same pull he has been following for months
- Decide whether entering Briarford is obedience, investigation, or both
- Understand what chose him, and why here, why now

**Possible checks**

- **Insight** — notice whether the pull feels like guidance, warning, or something wearing the shape of both
- **Perception** — track how many lanterns burn blue, whether any move or pulse, and whether anyone on the road is watching the treeline
- **Religion** or **Survival** — read whether the light belongs to consecrated ground, old regional faith, or something that does not fit any shrine you know

**Complication**

When the blue lantern pulses once — slow, like a breath — the force inside Mordain answers. His holy symbol grows warm against his sternum. For a heartbeat he knows exactly which path down the hill to take, whether he wants to or not.

- On a failed **Insight**, he misreads the sensation as certainty and commits before he has watched the village long enough
- On success, he can choose to follow the pull openly, test it by taking another path first, or use **Divine Sense** to learn whether anything unholy is tied to the light

**Clues to leave behind**

- At least one Briarford porch lantern burns blue while the rest hold ordinary flame
- A contract notice is visible on the village hall board even from the hillside: help wanted for unnatural lantern behavior
- The pull eases when Mordain faces downhill toward Briarford and tightens when he turns back to the forest

**Intro ending**

> You have watched long enough. The blue light does not dim. The village below is real, the road is clear, and the thing inside you has already decided you are going in. You leave the treeline at dusk, boots heavy on the path, and follow the warm windows until the tavern door stands open — and the mayor's hall is close enough to ask your questions before the lanterns change again.
