# Campaign Plan: When the Lanterns Fall Silent | Коли ліхтарі мовчать

> **Temporary draft** — working sketch for a 4–5 session beginner-friendly campaign.

---

## Current Scope

- **Campaign title:** When the Lanterns Fall Silent | Коли ліхтарі мовчать
- **Tone:** Heroic fantasy
- **Length:** 4 sessions (compressed) or **5 sessions (recommended for new groups)** — see split note below
- **Audience:** New group of friends/family
- **Starting setup:** The characters meet in a tavern after seeing contract posters in the village and a nearby city
- **Core promise:** A small village is suffering from strange lantern behavior, missing people, and something suspicious in the nearby forest

---

## Campaign Premise

A small village has been dealing with unusual lantern behavior. Some lanterns glow in impossible colors, flicker without wind, go dark at the same time each night, or appear in places where nobody hung them.

**What is really happening:** The lanterns are **captured souls** bound to serve the necromancer. They lead villagers from their homes toward the ruins. It is too risky for the necromancer to travel himself while he keeps the ritual going, and he needs a steady supply of villagers — the ritual was designed for a **circle of mages**, not one caster working alone.

The mayor has posted a contract asking for adventurers to investigate and deal with the problem. The contract is intentionally vague: it mentions "weird lantern behavior" and people going missing near houses where the lanterns act strangely, but it does not explain the deeper details.

A previous volunteer group already accepted the job but has not returned. The mayor is worried and **downplays the true scale of disappearances** — his main fault is hiding how many villagers are really gone, not orchestrating the crisis.

## Campaign Lore (resolved)

### The old prophecy and the seal

Something was sealed long ago — tied to an **old prophecy** the goblins feared ("dead light," "dead king," "lanterns go quiet"). **What exactly was sealed is not clear yet**; uncovering it is a main task for **campaign part 2**.

The current necromancer is trying to break that seal. Ages ago, magic at this scale required a **circle of mages**, not a single spellcaster. The ruins were one such place — a site of powerful ritual work. We do not know what ritual was performed there or its intended outcome, but the **devastating aftermath** is still visible regardless of whether the original magic was light or dark.

### Lanterns as bound souls

Village lanterns behaving strangely are not random curses — they are **souls captured to serve the necromancer**. They draw villagers from their homes and toward the ruins (where the party may find evidence of the led astray). Without fresh villagers, the necromancer grows too weak to sustain the ritual.

### Goblins and doom servants

The goblins in the coal mine **escaped the fate of other goblin groups** that now serve **doom servants** (name TBD). Little is known yet — only that the mine goblins were too afraid to stay in their former mountain homes and fled south. They are a symptom and a warning, not the final enemy.

### The amulet

The amulet on the goblin leader was **crafted long ago by a powerful circle of wizards** — not goblin work. The **Guild of Magic** in the nearby city knows more about it and will eventually try to **seize and seal it in their domain**. The party may learn the guild's intentions before showing the amulet, or recover it after seizure — **develop this line in campaign part 2**.

### Circles of mages (historical)

In the age when the ruins were active, both light and dark magic at high power required **multiple mages working in concert**. The necromancer's solo ritual is a desperate, incomplete echo of that old practice.

## Intended Session Flow

> **Pacing note — split mine and shrine:** Session 2 content (goblin coal mine + forgotten shrine) is **too dense for one table night with new players**. **Plan to run them as two separate sessions by default.** Only combine into a single session if the group moves very quickly and you are willing to trim mine rooms or shrine puzzles. See [Session 2 split (recommended)](#session-2-split-recommended) for end-of-session beats and numbering.

### 4-session track (compressed)

| Session | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **1** | Village investigation |
| **2** | Forest + coal mine + shrine *(only if pacing allows)* |
| **3** | Forgotten ruins |
| **4** | After the seal |

### 5-session track (recommended)

| Session | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **1** | Village investigation |
| **2** | Forest route and coal mine rescue |
| **3** | Forgotten shrine — herbs, riddle, rune map |
| **4** | Forgotten ruins — ritual finale |
| **5** | After the seal |

---

### Session 1: Village Investigation

The party arrives, meets in the tavern, gathers information around the village, investigates affected homes, uncovers a fake lantern scheme, handles an optional mill side quest, and learns that the next lead points into the nearby forest.

### Session 2: Coal Mines *(5-session track: Session 2)*

The party enters the forest, realizes the main city road will not lead to the real problem, follows the missing hunter's notes toward nearby coal mines, rescues blocked miners from a goblin incursion, and finds a strange non-goblin amulet on the goblin leader.

**End this session here** in the recommended 5-session track. Do not push on to the shrine the same night.

### Session 2B / Session 3: Forgotten Shrine *(5-session track: Session 3)*

The party follows the herbalist's notes to an old shrine where they gather magical herbs, solve a riddle, and the runes reveal a map to forgotten necromancer ruins.

In the compressed 4-session track, run mine + shrine back-to-back as a single Session 2 block.

### Session 3 / Session 4: Forgotten Ruins

After the shrine rune map, the party travels to the necromancer ruins, navigates magical darkness inside, solves a path riddle, learns lore in a library, and interrupts a timed ritual in the central chamber — rescuing (or failing) the missing villagers and unlocking the larger mystery.

### Session 4 / Session 5: Return to the Village

**End session 4 here** — cliffhanger or aftermath at the ruins — so players have time between sessions to read handouts, think about builds, and process the finale.

**Start session 5** with level-up: a **short rest** at the village, or a visit to a specific NPC who helps them consolidate what they learned (tie to reward scene).

The party returns to Briarford to collect payment and closure. **The village reflects what happened at the ruins:**

| Ritual outcome | Village state | Campaign part 2 hook |
|----------------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Seal broken** (ritual completed) | Village **transformed** — visible wrongness, changed NPCs or environment | Bigger adventure: what the prophecy unleashed |
| **Ritual stopped** (concentration broken) | Party wrapped in **magical mist** ordinary villagers fear; mayor **hesitates to reward** and may **blame them as cursed** | Exiles or outcasts heading to the city |

**Session 5 beats:**

1. Travel back from ruins (short scene; diary in pack, amulet in hand)
2. **Level up** — short rest or mentor NPC; time to read diary handout and plan builds
3. Confront Mayor Alden for reward — his reaction depends on outcome (grateful but nervous vs. fearful and accusatory)
4. **Map writer NPC** — party shows necromancer diary; he **cannot read it** and is **visibly terrified**, but confirms it is serious
5. Map writer directs them to the **city** — home of the **Guild of Magic** and many other guilds; the guild may help (and may want the amulet)
6. Closing image — see [Campaign ending images](#campaign-ending-images)

## Session 1 Goals

By the end of session 1, the players should:

1. Understand that the lantern problem is **real** (bound souls) but some villagers are **also** exploiting the fear with fakes
2. Meet important village NPCs
3. Learn that people have gone missing
4. Discover that the herbalist noticed strange lights in the forest before disappearing
5. Learn that the previous volunteer group may have followed the same trail
6. Decide to head into the forest
7. **Individual intro scenes** play first; everyone **converges at the tavern** where **Mayor Alden** is present (hire, rumors, or official welcome)

## Opening Scene: Tavern Meeting

Each player gets a **5–10 minute intro scene** before the group fully gathers. Intros teach RP and one rule at a time. **All intros end at the tavern**, where the party meets and **Mayor Alden** is already present (drinking, posting follow-ups, or interviewing hirelings).

The characters may already know each other, or they may each have come because of the posted contract.

### Individual Character Introductions

The players want to build their characters themselves, so session 1 should support short individual introductions before the party fully gathers. Each introduction should connect the character's backstory, class, or personal goal to the same village contract.

Use these as **5–10 minute spotlight scenes**. They do not need to be full encounters. A single skill check, quick combat, discovery, or roleplay moment is enough.

**Design goals:**

- Let each player feel their character has a personal reason to care
- Teach one basic rule at a time for new players
- Avoid forcing a specific backstory before players choose one
- End each intro with a reason to visit the village and ask about the lantern contract
- Bring everyone together at the tavern or mayor's house before the main investigation begins

#### Example: Hunter / Ranger Introduction

The hunter or ranger is in the forest gathering meat, checking snares, or tracking game near the village.

They discover a stranger's body that has been attacked by a wolf or wolf-like creature. This can be handled as:

- A quick fight against one wounded wolf
- A single attack or skill roll if the group wants a faster start
- A tracking/survival scene where the creature has already fled

After dealing with the creature, the character searches the body and finds a copy of the village contract. The dead stranger may have been heading to Briarford to help, or may have been connected to the missing volunteer group.

**Clues on the body:**

- A contract poster folded into a pocket
- A travel pack with oil, rope, and a half-burned lantern wick
- Scratch marks that look animal-made, but faint blue ash around the wounds suggests something stranger
- A note with the village name and "ask the mayor before nightfall"

**Intro ending:**

> With meat on your shoulder, a dead stranger at your feet, and a contract in your hand, the village no longer feels like someone else's problem.

#### Other Character Introduction Templates

Create similar introductions once the players share their characters:

| Class / archetype | Hook |
|-------------------|------|
| **Cleric / Paladin** | Sent by a temple after prayers or lantern flames in the shrine begin behaving strangely |
| **Wizard / Sorcerer / Warlock** | Notices a magical pattern in the lantern reports or senses unusual blue light tied to old magic |
| **Rogue / Bard** | Comes across the contract in the city, hears rumors that frightened homes are being robbed, or knows one of the fake-lantern villagers |
| **Fighter / Barbarian** | Hired as caravan protection on the road to the village, then sees signs of danger near the forest road |
| **Druid** | Feels animals acting strangely near the woods and follows the disturbance toward the village |
| **Artificer / Tinker-style** | Interested in reports of lanterns behaving impossibly, especially if one may be fake or modified |

These should stay flexible until the player characters are known.

### Contract Poster

Suggested text:

> Adventurers, guards, hunters, and brave souls wanted.
>
> The village of Briarford seeks aid with unnatural lantern behavior and recent disappearances. Fair payment offered upon investigation and resolution.
>
> Speak with Mayor Alden at the village hall.

### Tavern Rumors

Use these to guide new players toward investigation without overwhelming them:

- "My neighbor swears her porch lantern whispered her name."
- "The mayor hired volunteers last week. Haven't seen them since."
- "Lanterns near the old road burn blue after midnight."
- "The herbalist kept walking toward the forest at night. Said he was watching lights."
- "Some folk are using this panic as an excuse to rob empty homes."

### Map Writer NPC *(name TBD — integrate player backstories later)*

Plant this NPC in session 1. Once you have all player characters, consider tying one or more PCs to the map writer through backstory.

**Suggested placement:** tavern regular — a **cartographer / map maker** passing through or living in Briarford, not merely a scribe.

**Who they are:**

- **Creates maps** of the region; can read shrine-script and old road-marks well enough to **copy** them, but is not a master linguist
- Ink-stained fingers, half-finished map scroll, measuring tools
- Personal hook TBD when players are known (lost family, herbalist's friend, etc.)

**Session 1 touchpoints:**

- Tavern rumor: "The map writer in the corner copies every road-mark the mayor ignores"
- If befriended: offers to sketch routes — "bring me what you find in the woods"

**Session 3 role (shrine map):**

- If the party **fails** to read the rune map, they need the map writer to **trace and translate the route** from a rubbing or copy

**Session 5 role (diary):**

- Party shows the **necromancer's diary** — the map writer **cannot read the language** and is **deeply frightened**
- He will **not** solve the mystery here; he points the party to the **city** and the **Guild of Magic** (and mentions other guilds there that might matter later)

## Key Village Locations

### Mayor's House / Village Hall

The mayor can officially hire the group and provide payment terms. At first, he gives only basic information.

**Character:** Sneaky and cautious — he does **not** want to raise alarm. He genuinely believes the trouble is **bandits, petty theft, or a small local curse**, not an undead conspiracy. His main failing is **hiding the real scale of missing villagers** (how many, how fast), not knowing the full truth.

**What he withholds:**

- The true number of disappearances
- How badly the village is bleeding population

**Useful information he can reveal if pressured or approached after investigation:**

- A volunteer group investigated recently and did not return
- The missing group last asked about the herbalist
- The first affected lantern was near the forest-facing road
- The hunter disappeared after checking animal tracks near the mill and woods

**After the ruins (session 5):**

- If the **seal was broken:** village transformed; mayor's authority may be gone or irrelevant
- If the **ritual was stopped:** mayor sees the party wreathed in **feared magical mist** — **hesitates to pay**, may **blame them as cursed** and turn the village against them

### Affected Houses

*TBD — define specific homes after village map and world map are drafted.*

The party should investigate a couple of homes where lanterns behaved strangely and people went missing or fled. Clues should support the **soul-lantern** truth: lanterns that move, whisper, or lead people toward the forest road.

### House with the Fake Lantern

One affected house contains a fake lantern planted by badly behaved villagers. These villagers planned to scare people away from homes so they could rob them.

**Placeholder NPCs *(flesh out later, low priority)*:**

| Name | Role |
|------|------|
| **Bram Keswick** | Ringleader; copied "real" blue flicker he saw near the forest road |
| **Tessa Aldridge** | Neighbor who helped plant the fake lantern; nervous if questioned |
| **Old Henk** | Fence for stolen goods; hides loot in a shed behind the cooper's yard |

**This should reveal that:**

- Not every "weird lantern" report is magical
- Some locals are exploiting the situation
- The real mystery still exists

**Possible evidence:**

- The lantern has a hidden compartment with colored glass or alchemical powder
- The flame smell is chemical, not like normal oil
- Bootprints match local villagers rather than unknown creatures
- Stolen goods are hidden nearby

**Suggested encounter:**

- Social confrontation first
- If the players push or catch the thieves in the act, it can become a short non-lethal fight

### Herbalist's House

The herbalist is missing. His diary is the strongest clue leading toward the forest.

**Important discoveries:**

- The herbalist noticed strange lights in the forest
- He believed the lights were moving in a pattern
- He may have suspected the lantern behavior was connected to old forest magic
- His last diary entry suggests he went to observe the lights more closely

**Diary excerpt:**

> Third night of the blue lights. They are not fireflies, not will-o'-wisps, and not lanterns carried by human hands. They move as if answering each other.
>
> The village lanterns flickered again tonight, always when the forest lights gathered near the old trail.
>
> I will follow at dawn. If I am right, the source is not in the village.

### Miller's Mill

**Optional** — only if the party visits the mill and speaks with the miller. Abnormal rats have infested the mill. He would normally ask the hunter for help, but the hunter has gone missing.

**Purpose of the side quest:**

- Give new players a simple combat encounter
- Teach basic initiative, attacks, movement, and skill checks
- Plant another clue about the forest

**Possible rat details:**

- The rats have faint glowing eyes
- They are unusually coordinated
- They gnawed at sacks in spiral shapes
- They avoid lantern light, except when it flickers blue

**Possible reward:**

- A small payment
- Food supplies
- A lantern oil flask
- The miller's trust and extra information

**Magic lantern hook (sessions 1–3):**

- Optional: the miller keeps an old **mine lantern** that once burned with a faint blue edge (same detail as the broken mining lantern in session 2). It no longer lights on its own, but a spellcaster can recharge it with *light* or similar magic for one long rest's worth of reliable illumination
- Teaches players that normal torches may not be enough later — without forcing a fetch quest

**Clue from the mill:**

- The hunter found strange tracks behind the mill before disappearing
- The tracks led toward the forest edge

## Session 2: Coal Mines and Forgotten Shrine

### Session 2 Goals

By the end of session 2, the players should:

1. Understand that the village problem is connected to something deeper in the forest
2. Investigate the coal mines mentioned in the missing hunter's notes
3. Discover that goblins have returned to the region after a long absence
4. Rescue surviving miners or learn what happened to them
5. Defeat or drive off a goblin leader speaking about coming doom
6. Find a strange carved amulet that was not made by goblins
7. Visit the old shrine from the herbalist's notes
8. Learn that the next major destination is a set of forgotten ruins tied to an ancient necromancer

### Forest Entry

The party enters the forest after session 1. It should be obvious that simply following the main road toward the city will not solve the problem.

**Ways to point them toward the correct path:**

- The mayor gives them the missing hunter's notes before they leave
- The hunter's notes mention strange signs around the nearest coal mines
- Animal tracks and disturbed undergrowth veer away from the main road
- Blue lantern-like lights flicker between trees in the direction of the mines
- The herbalist's diary mentions both the old shrine and forest paths away from the road

**Hunter note excerpt:**

> Tracks behind the mill are wrong. Too many small feet. Not rats, not children. Old prints near the coal road too.
>
> Lantern-light seen between the pines, but no human steps beside it.
>
> Coal mine first. Shrine after, if I still have daylight.

### Coal Mine Discovery

When the party reaches the coal mines, they find signs of violence.

**First image:**

> The trees thin around a black wound in the hillside. The mine entrance stands half-collapsed, its support beams hacked and splintered. A few miners have been piled on crude improvised spears, their lanterns broken at their feet.

**Investigation details:**

- The spears are crude, but deliberately placed to frighten people away
- Small footprints, clawed hands, and rough blade marks suggest goblins
- The bodies are miners, not soldiers
- Some blood trails lead deeper into the mine, suggesting other miners may still be alive
- A broken mining lantern burns with a faint blue edge before going out

**Important context:**

- Goblins have not been seen here for a long time
- They **fled south** from their former mountain homes — **escaping the fate of other goblin groups** now serving **doom servants** (details unknown; campaign part 2)
- They are panicked, displaced, and afraid of the old prophecy — not a confident invasion force
- The amulet they carry is **not theirs**; they fear it

### Mine Crawl — volunteer group discovery

En route or at the mine entrance, the party can find **the previous volunteer group — dead**. This confirms the forest trail is lethal and connects session 1 rumors to session 2 stakes.

### Mine Crawl

The mine should feel dangerous but manageable for new players.

**Possible areas:**

1. **Entrance Tunnels** — broken carts, coal dust, warning signs, first goblin scouts
2. **Collapsed Side Shaft** — trapped miner sounds or a risky shortcut
3. **Lantern Gallery** — hanging mine lanterns flicker blue when goblins approach
4. **Storage Room** — tools, rope, lamp oil, and maybe a frightened miner hiding
5. **Deep Work Chamber** — captured miners and the goblin leader

**Possible encounters:**

- 2 goblin scouts arguing over stolen food
- A simple trap: loose stones, tripwire, falling coal basket, or noise alarm
- A blocked passage that can be cleared with Athletics, tools, or clever thinking
- Captured miners guarded by goblins

Keep combat simple. This is a good place to teach cover, darkvision, light, short movement choices, and helping an ally.

### Captured Miners

Some miners are still alive, blocked or captured deeper inside.

**They can tell the party:**

- The goblins came suddenly from lower tunnels or an old breach
- The goblins were not acting like normal raiders
- The goblin leader kept shouting about doom, debt, and "the dead light"
- One goblin carried an amulet the others seemed afraid to touch
- Something deeper than the goblins may have driven them into the mine

### Goblin Leader

The goblin leader should be frightening but not too complex mechanically.

**Personality:**

- Panicked fanatic rather than confident warlord
- Speaks in broken threats and prophecy fragments
- Believes doom is coming and that the goblins had to flee south

**Possible lines:**

- "Stone-roots crack. Mountain-home screams."
- "Dead light wakes. Dead king calls."
- "You kill me, doom still walks."
- "Lanterns go quiet. Then bones remember."

After the goblin leader is defeated, the party finds a strange carved amulet.

### Strange Amulet

The amulet is definitely not goblin work. It was **made long ago by a powerful circle of wizards** — precise runes, old binding craft.

**Description:**

- Dark stone or old bone, polished smooth
- Carved with precise runes too careful for goblin hands
- Cold even near fire
- Shows a lantern shape with no flame, or a skull hidden inside a lantern pattern
- Gives a faint pull toward the deeper forest

**Campaign part 2:** The **Guild of Magic** in the city knows more and will try to **seize and seal** the amulet in their vaults. Party may uncover guild intentions early or steal the amulet back after seizure — **develop later**.

**Possible checks:**

| Skill | Reveal |
|-------|--------|
| **Arcana** | The carving is tied to necromancy, binding, or old death magic |
| **History** | The style is older than the village and not local goblin craft |
| **Religion** | The symbol feels like a warning against restless dead |
| **Investigation** | The amulet has been carried recently but made long ago |

The amulet should raise questions, not answer them.

### Old Forgotten Shrine

The next clue points to an old shrine deep in the woods. The herbalist's notes say this was one of his regular gathering places for rare magical herbs.

**Shrine purpose:**

- Provide a calmer discovery scene after the mine fight
- Reward investigation and nature knowledge
- Reveal a larger map toward the next destination
- Give the party a small magical boon before deeper danger

**Herbalist note excerpt:**

> Shrine moss strong this season. Silverleaf under the broken arch can draw poison from blood if cut after moonrise.
>
> Do not take the young shoots. Green-veined leaves are not ready. Bitter poison until the white edge forms.
>
> Old stones still answer riddles if touched with clean flame.

### Magical Herbs

The shrine has rare herbs able to heal even deadly poison if gathered correctly.

**Possible herb:**

- **Silverleaf** — mature leaves have pale white edges and a cool scent
- **Young silverleaf** — green-veined and sharp-smelling; poisonous if used

**Check:**

- **Nature or Survival:** identify mature herbs
- On success, the party gathers a useful antidote herb
- On failure, they gather immature herbs that are poisonous unless corrected later
- On a close failure, they gather both and must sort them out with another clue or check

Keep consequences beginner-friendly:

- The poisonous herbs should create risk later, not instantly ruin the session
- A careful player can avoid the danger by asking questions, comparing notes, or using magic

### Shrine Riddle

A riddle is hidden among the ruined stones. Solving it lets the party add a poison effect to one weapon.

**Suggested riddle theme:**

> I sleep in leaf and root below,
> I heal when named, I kill when grown.
> I wear the moon along my blade,
> I turn the hunter's debt repaid.
>
> What am I?

**Answer idea:** Silverleaf or the white-edged leaf.

**Reward:**

- One weapon can be treated with shrine poison
- Keep it simple: once during the next combat, the weapon deals extra poison damage or forces a low DC Constitution save against poison
- Make it feel like a one-use blessing from the shrine rather than a permanent upgrade

### Shrine Rune Map

After the riddle is solved, the shrine runes shift into a map.

**What the map shows:**

- The village
- The coal mine
- The old shrine
- A path deeper through the forest
- A set of forgotten ruins where a powerful necromancer lived ages ago

The map should make the next step clear: the party should head to the forgotten ruins.

**Possible closing image:**

> The shrine stones grind softly as blue light fills their cracks. Lines crawl across the old rock, forming roads, trees, and a black mark deep in the woods. Beneath that mark, one rune burns brighter than the rest: a lantern without a flame.

### Session 2 split (recommended)

**Default plan:** treat the goblin mine and the forgotten shrine as **two separate sessions**, not one long forest day.

| Block | Run as | Ends when |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| **Coal mine** | Session 2 (5-session track) | Goblin leader defeated; amulet found; miners rescued or mourned |
| **Forgotten shrine** | Session 3 (5-session track) | Rune map revealed; party knows the route to the ruins |

**Why split:**

- Mine crawl alone can fill 2–3 hours (scouts, trap, trapped miners, boss fight, debrief)
- Shrine is a different pace — exploration, Nature checks, riddle, reward — and deserves a fresh start
- New players need time to absorb combat rules in the mine before adding puzzle pressure at the shrine
- Ending on the amulet cold-pull and "an unseen lantern goes dark" is a strong cliffhanger into shrine night

**Mine-only session ending (use this as the stop point):**

> The last goblin falls. The rescued miners whisper prayers. In your hand, the strange amulet turns cold, and somewhere deeper in the forest, an unseen lantern goes dark.

**Shrine session opening:** Short recap — travel from mine camp or forest trail; herbalist notes and amulet pull them toward the old stones. No need to replay the mine fight.

**Compressed alternative (4-session track only):** Run forest entry, full mine, and full shrine in one session if the table is fast and you trim optional mine rooms. End with the rune map pointing toward the necromancer ruins. Expect a long night.

## Session 4: Forgotten Ruins *(5-session track)*

*In the compressed 4-session track, this is Session 3.*

### Session 4 Goals

By the end of session 4, the players should:

1. Reach the necromancer ruins using the shrine rune map (or map writer help if they could not read it)
2. Find the hidden entrance and survive the magically dark interior
3. Solve a navigation riddle or pay the cost of a wrong turn
4. Learn what the necromancer was trying to unlock (old prophecy, circle-of-mages history, goblin fears)
5. Resolve the ritual — **all captives die either way** as souls are drained for energy
6. Leave with the diary, amulet, and loot — **level up happens at start of session 5**

**End session 4 at the ruins** (or on the road back) so players can read handouts and plan builds before session 5.

### Shrine Map → Travel

The rune map from session 2 shows the route to the ruins. Before the party can follow it reliably:

| Outcome | What happens |
|---------|----------------|
| **Success** (e.g. Arcana, History, or Investigation — set DC for your table) | The party reads the marks: trail landmarks, distance, and a warning symbol near the ruins |
| **Failure** | The map is clearly important but the script is unreadable. They need the **map writer NPC** from session 1, or must return to the village for help copying the route |

**Travel beat:** Short overland scene — forest deepens, animal silence, old stones visible between roots. Optional: one navigation check using the translated map.

### Arrival: No Visible Entrance

The ruins look like a collapsed hillside or broken wall — no obvious door.

**Sensory hook:**

> The air tastes of iron and old ash. No birds. No insects. Somewhere under the rubble, magic hums like a held breath.

**Finding the entrance:**

| Approach | Result |
|----------|--------|
| ***Detect magic*** (or similar) | Reveals a magical aura concentrated in what looks like a collapsed wall; faint outline of steps descending behind fallen stone |
| **Hard exploration roll** (e.g. Investigation or Perception, higher DC) | Party notices worn steps, scrape marks, or a draft — the "wall" is a disguised collapse covering the true entry |
| **Failure** | They can camp, retry, or follow sounds from deeper inside (risk a random encounter or lost time) |

### Interior: Magical Darkness

Inside the ruins, **normal torches and lanterns provide only dim, unreliable light** — enough to see a few feet, not enough to fight or navigate safely.

**Solutions (any one works; reward prior prep):**

| Source | Where planted |
|--------|----------------|
| ***Light*** cantrip / *daylight* / similar | Any spellcaster |
| **Magic lantern from session 1** | Miller's old mine lantern (rechargeable) |
| **Magic lantern from session 2** | Broken mining lantern with blue edge — can be restored or used as a focus |
| **Magic lantern from session 3** | Found in an early side room, shrine-touched herb oil, or loot from the library |

Design intent: teach light and darkness rules once; do not punish a group that only has *light* if someone prepared it.

### Path Riddle (Navigation)

A junction or inscribed hall forces a choice: which way leads to the **center** (ritual chamber) vs. a dead end or trap wing.

**Suggested format:**

- Verse on the wall referencing the shrine silverleaf riddle, lanterns without flame, or "the path that feeds the root, not the branch"
- **Correct path** → library or anteroom before the ritual hall
- **Wrong path** → short encounter: **1–2 skeletons and a ghoul** (or scaled for party). Enough to teach undead traits, not a full resource drain

**Contingency:** Repeated failures cost time — see ritual timer below.

### Library (Knowledge Encounter)

If the party reaches the library (via correct riddle path or thorough exploration):

**Discoveries:**

- Ruins were a **circle-of-mages ritual site**; devastating aftermath still visible
- The village lantern disturbances are **bound soul-lanterns** leading victims toward the ruins
- Ritual notes (partial): something tied to the **old prophecy** was **sealed** long ago; goblin "dead light" fragments refer to this
- **Magic books** with unknown symbols — same class of script as the diary; unreadable now
- **Scrolls** the party *can* use (healing, protection, or one offensive spell) — intended to help in the final fight

Keep exposition interactive: skill checks reveal more; players choose what to pocket before pushing on.

### Ritual Chamber (Finale)

**Scene image:**

> A circular chamber. Chains, circles, and guttering blue flame. Villagers — missing from Briarford — hang or kneel at the edges, drained and fading. At the center, a robed figure chants over a seal carved into the floor, eyes on the pattern, not on you. The necromancer cannot leave the circle easily; **soul-lanterns** did the work of luring prey here.

#### Timed Ritual

The necromancer is **focused on the ritual** and does not attack directly while concentrating. **Minions** (skeletons, ghouls, or 1–2 stronger undead) protect him at all costs.

**Timer (tune to your table):**

| Round / phase | Ritual progress |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Start | Necromancer concentrating; minions between party and center |
| Mid | Seal cracks; blue light pulses; one villager dies or transforms if you want pressure |
| Final phase | Seal nearly open — last chance to break concentration |

**Reasonable default for new players:** 4–6 rounds until completion, or 3 failed concentration saves from the necromancer (if you run it that way). Wrong-path detours should cost 1–2 "phases" of progress.

#### Two Outcomes

| Event | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Concentration broken** (damage, *counterspell*, killing minions and reaching him, etc.) | Ritual **canceled**. Necromancer joins the fight personally — wounded, angry, but still dangerous |
| **Ritual completes** | Necromancer **dies** as all his power drains into unlocking the seal. Minions may collapse or flee. The seal opens — **village transformation hook (session 5)** |

Either outcome should feel like a win with consequences, not a fail state.

**Captives:** When the ritual ends — **either way** — the villagers are **dead**. The necromancer **drained their souls for energy**. There is no last-minute rescue; the party fights to stop the seal, avenge the taken, or both.

#### After the Fight

Regardless of how the ritual resolved:

- Search the necromancer's body → **diary** in an unknown language (brief ritual description inside)
- **Uncommon item** (wand, amulet variant, or lantern focus — tie to lantern theme)
- **No level up yet** — save for **session 5** opening (short rest or NPC visit in village)
- Return to Briarford for reward and fallout

### Campaign ending images

Use **two beats** to bridge into campaign part 2:

1. **The necromancer's diary** — physical prop or handout; unknown language; map writer recoils
2. **Village transformation** — depends on ritual outcome (see session 5 table)

**If seal broken:**

> Briarford's windows glow wrong colors. The mayor's hall is empty. In the square, a lantern floats with no hand to hold it — and turns to watch you leave.

**If ritual stopped (mist curse):**

> The mayor will not touch your coin. Villagers cross the street. Around your boots, pale mist coils, and every hearth-fire dims as you pass.

### Magic Lantern Tracker (DM prep)

Use this checklist so darkness prep pays off:

| Session | Lantern hook |
|---------|----------------|
| **1** | Miller's rechargeable mine lantern (optional reward) |
| **2** | Blue-edged broken lantern at mine entrance; amulet cold near ruins |
| **3** | Side-room lantern, library note about "flame that obeys spell, not oil" |

A party with none of these should still succeed via *light* — lanterns are a bonus, not a hard gate.

## Feedback and Design Notes

- This is a strong beginner-friendly start because it gives the players a clear job, a safe social hub, an investigation, and an optional simple fight
- The fake lantern subplot teaches players that not every clue has the same source — while the **true** lanterns are necromancer-bound souls
- The mayor hiding the **scale** of disappearances gives the party reason to dig; he is sneaky but not the villain — he thinks bandits or a small curse
- The mill rat quest is a good rules tutorial. Keep it short so it supports the investigation instead of replacing it
- The herbalist diary should be the clearest forest hook. New players often appreciate at least one direct lead
- Session 2 may be too dense for a new group if both the mine rescue and shrine puzzle are played in full — **split them into two sessions by default** (5-session track); see [Session 2 split (recommended)](#session-2-split-recommended)
- The goblins should feel like a symptom, not the final cause. Their return from the far northern mountain roots is a mystery that can point toward the larger campaign
- The amulet and shrine map give two strong clues toward the forgotten necromancer ruins, so the party should not feel stuck even if they miss some mine details
- Session 3 is the campaign climax: timed ritual, moral weight of villagers, and two valid outcomes (fight the necromancer vs. watch him burn out opening the seal)
- Plant the **map writer NPC** in session 1; integrate player backstories when PCs are known
- **Magic lanterns** across sessions 1–3 reward optional mill visit; always allow *light* as the baseline solution
- Wrong-path skeleton/ghoul fights should be short — the ritual timer is the real pressure
- **Level up between sessions:** end session 4 at ruins or road home; level up at session 5 open so players can read diary and plan builds
- Captives cannot be saved — emotional stake is stopping the seal and learning what was taken
