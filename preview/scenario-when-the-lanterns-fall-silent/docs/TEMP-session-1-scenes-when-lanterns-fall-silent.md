# Session 1 scenes draft: When the Lanterns Fall Silent

> Draft planning document. This is a scene outline only, not final `dm-script`
> content. Source material: `docs/TEMP-campaign-when-lanterns-fall-silent.md`
> and the current character intro drafts in
> `scenarios/when-the-lanterns-fall-silent/dm-script/`.

## Session 1 intent

Session 1 should move from personal hooks into a shared village investigation.
By the end, the party should know that Briarford's lantern problem is real,
that some villagers are exploiting the panic, that multiple people are missing,
and that the strongest trail leads into the forest.

Recommended pacing for a new group:

1. Run short individual intro spotlights.
2. Converge at the Briarford tavern with Mayor Alden present or quickly summoned.
3. Offer a small investigation menu, not every possible node.
4. End once the party chooses the forest lead.

## Scene list

### Scene 0 - Table framing and contract handout

**Purpose:** Give players a shared premise before the solo spotlights.

**Draft notes:**

- Show or paraphrase the same contract text used in each intro:
  Briarford seeks help with unnatural lantern behavior and recent
  disappearances; speak with Mayor Alden; fair payment offered.
- Explain that the first few scenes are short personal spotlights and everyone
  will meet in the village tavern.
- Keep the "captured souls" truth hidden. At this point the problem is rumors,
  fear, and a paid job.

**Useful handout seed:**

> Adventurers, guards, hunters, and brave souls wanted. The village of
> Briarford seeks aid with unnatural lantern behavior and recent disappearances.
> Fair payment offered upon investigation and resolution. Speak with Mayor
> Alden at the village hall.

### Scene 1A - Vex / Harry Potter: The Wrong Name

**Type:** Solo intro, 5-10 minutes.

**Purpose:** Give Vex a reason to flee toward Briarford and teach Insight,
optional unarmed strike, and Stealth.

**Current source:** `01_intro_vex.en.md`

**Draft notes:**

- Location: The Last Lamp, a tavern on the Briarford road outside the city.
- Inciting beat: Tomas Grull says "Vex" aloud and mentions the vanished
  Ashmere crew.
- Main check: Insight DC 12 to decide whether Vex reads Tomas as a hunter or a
  scared former runner.
- Player-facing stakes: protect the real name, decide whether to flee, bluff,
  rob, or talk outside.
- Exit state: Vex heads for Briarford under the public name Harry Potter.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Porch lanterns may whisper names.
- Briarford's contract has city/guild money behind it.
- The Ashmere/ledger thread may connect to sealed city records later.

**Tavern convergence hook:** In Briarford, "Harry Potter" recognizes the same
contract notice and may stay guarded if anyone mentions whispered names.

### Scene 1B - Ruta: The Cat and the Blue Light

**Type:** Solo intro, 5-10 minutes.

**Purpose:** Give Ruta a warm, ranger-flavored hook and teach Animal Handling,
Survival, and Perception.

**Current source:** `05_intro_ruta.en.md`

**Draft notes:**

- Location: Mossy clearing off the Briarford forest road.
- Inciting beat: a ginger tom steals dried fish, then reacts before Ruta does
  to a wrong blue light in the trees.
- Main check: Animal Handling DC 11 to coax the cat; Perception DC 12 to read
  the blue lantern and contract marker.
- Tone should stay lightly comic, with the cat acting as compass rather than
  magical answer.
- Exit state: Ruta follows the contract marker and/or the cat toward
  Briarford.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- One porch lantern burns blue while nearby lights remain ordinary.
- Animals react to the pulse before people do.
- Fresh city-boot tracks cross the forest-edge clearing.
- Optional second out-of-sync lantern matches other PCs' observations.

**Tavern convergence hook:** The cat can reappear on the tavern porch for a
small social beat.

### Scene 1C - Mordain: Before the Threshold

**Type:** Solo intro, 5-10 minutes.

**Purpose:** Give Mordain a personal spiritual contradiction and teach Insight
plus Divine Sense.

**Current source:** `04_intro_mordain.en.md`

**Draft notes:**

- Location: Treeline above Briarford at dusk.
- Inciting beat: the blue lantern pulse makes Mordain's holy symbol warm and
  gives him path-knowledge toward the village.
- Main check: Insight DC 12 to read the pull as both guidance and warning, not
  simple certainty.
- Optional teach moment: Divine Sense halfway down the hill can reveal undead
  tied to the blue lantern and desecrated ground on the approach road.
- Exit state: Mordain reaches the tavern or mayor's hall with the sense that
  the light and his calling overlap dangerously.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Blue lantern pulse is not ordinary flame.
- The approach road may be desecrated.
- Undead are tied to the blue lantern network, but do not explain bound souls
  yet.
- Old way-shrine phrase: "Dead light sees the road."

**Tavern convergence hook:** Mordain can contrast his certainty/pull with the
scholars' measurements and Vex's name-whisper rumor.

### Scene 1D - Lilith: Blue Measurements

**Type:** Solo intro, 5-10 minutes.

**Purpose:** Give Lilith direct magical evidence and teach Arcana,
Investigation, and careful reagent handling.

**Current source:** `02_intro_lilith.en.md`

**Draft notes:**

- Location: Forest edge above Briarford, near a ring of herbs that glow under
  the blue lantern pulse.
- Inciting beat: a porch lantern flares, a name-shaped whisper rides the pulse,
  and Lilith's focus cracks.
- Main checks: Investigation DC 12 or Arcana DC 13 for herb gathering; Arcana
  DC 12 on the flare.
- Let careful play earn at least one sample without blocking the hook.
- Exit state: Lilith enters the tavern with samples, notes, and evidence that
  the village is broadcasting something.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Herbs react to Briarford blue lantern light, not ordinary fire.
- Magical background spikes toward the village.
- The pulse carries information shaped like a name.
- Optional second lantern flickers out of sync.

**Tavern convergence hook:** Lilith can compare notes with Elias and test
whether tavern rumors match her measured pulse.

### Scene 1E - Elias: The Same Colour Twice

**Type:** Solo intro, 5-10 minutes.

**Purpose:** Give Elias a research/personal-history hook and teach Arcana,
History, and optional social investigation.

**Current source:** `03_intro_elias.en.md`

**Draft notes:**

- Location: The Blue Wick, an inn on the Briarford road.
- Inciting beat: Elias compares witness accounts, sees a window lantern flicker
  blue, then a peddler's pack lantern flares the same color.
- Main checks: History DC 11 or Arcana DC 12 to reconcile accounts; Arcana
  DC 12 on the flare.
- Personal layer: the color matches the wilderness encounter that awakened his
  sorcerous power.
- Exit state: Elias leaves before dawn for Briarford with at least two clues
  and a reason to test the phenomenon himself.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Witnesses describe blue light pulsing like breath.
- Journal fragments mention dead light in old songs.
- City guild posting fees connect to Mayor Alden's contract.
- Optional name-shaped whispers match Vex and Lilith.

**Tavern convergence hook:** Elias and Lilith should quickly realize they are
describing the same impossible blue from different angles.

### Scene 2 - Briarford tavern convergence

**Type:** Group scene.

**Purpose:** Put all PCs at the same table, introduce Mayor Alden, and turn
personal clues into a shared investigation.

**Draft notes:**

- Location: village tavern, warm but nervous. Contract copied on the wall.
- Mayor Alden can already be present, drinking while pretending not to worry,
  or summoned by the barkeep once armed strangers ask about the contract.
- Let each PC share one clue from their intro. Do not require all secrets to be
  public; Vex can keep the real name hidden, Mordain can keep the divine pull
  private, Elias can conceal his wilderness memory.
- Introduce the map writer as a tavern regular if possible. This plants the
  later shrine/diary support without making him solve session 1.

**Mayor Alden draft notes:**

- He is cautious and evasive, not the villain.
- He thinks the problem may be bandits, theft, or a small curse.
- His main fault is hiding the scale and pace of disappearances.
- If pressured now, he should reveal one useful lead but not everything.

**Rumors to seed:**

- "My neighbor swears her porch lantern whispered her name."
- "The mayor hired volunteers last week. Haven't seen them since."
- "Lanterns near the old road burn blue after midnight."
- "The herbalist kept walking toward the forest at night."
- "Some folk are using this panic as an excuse to rob empty homes."

**Decision point:** Ask where the party investigates first. Offer 2-3 obvious
options: affected homes, herbalist's house, mill, mayor's hall.

### Scene 3 - Mayor's hall / official briefing

**Type:** Social investigation.

**Purpose:** Establish the job terms, Alden's evasiveness, and the missing
people stakes.

**Draft notes:**

- This can happen before or after the tavern depending on player choices.
- Alden gives payment terms and basic facts: unnatural lantern behavior,
  recent disappearances, a previous volunteer group, and a missing herbalist.
- He downplays how many people are missing. Let Insight catch the evasion.
- If the party is respectful, he points them to affected homes and the
  herbalist. If they pressure him, he admits the previous volunteers asked
  about the herbalist and/or hunter.

**Possible checks:**

- Insight to notice he is minimizing the scale.
- Persuasion or Intimidation to get one extra fact.
- Investigation on records or notice board to spot missing names, dates, or
  repeated forest-road addresses.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Previous volunteer group did not return.
- Herbalist and hunter are both missing.
- First affected lantern was near the forest-facing road.
- Alden is hiding scale, not orchestrating the crisis.

### Scene 4 - Affected homes: real lantern evidence

**Type:** Investigation scene.

**Purpose:** Prove the true lantern phenomenon is real before or alongside the
fake-lantern subplot.

**Draft notes:**

- Use one or two homes, not a whole street, unless players love investigation.
- Details should point toward the soul-lantern truth without naming it:
  doors left open, lantern soot or blue ash, cold hearth, name-whisper stories,
  tracks toward the forest road.
- Let the party compare their intro clues here: Lilith's pulse notes, Elias's
  breath rhythm, Mordain's undead sense, Ruta's animal reaction, Vex's rumor.

**Possible checks:**

- Investigation for physical traces.
- Arcana for impossible flame behavior.
- Perception for tracks or a flicker at another house.
- Religion or Divine Sense if Mordain tests the porch lantern up close.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Real lantern events lead victims toward the forest road.
- The lights behave like coordinated signals, not isolated bad oil.
- Missing people leave ordinary homes without signs of forced entry.

### Scene 5 - Fake lantern house and local thieves

**Type:** Investigation/social confrontation, optional short non-lethal fight.

**Purpose:** Teach that not every report has the same cause while keeping the
real mystery alive.

**Draft notes:**

- Suspects from the campaign draft: Bram Keswick, Tessa Aldridge, Old Henk.
- They copied real blue flicker behavior to scare people away from homes and
  steal from them.
- Keep the encounter local and petty. They are criminals exploiting fear, not
  necromancer agents.
- This scene works best after the party has seen at least one real clue, so the
  fake reveal does not deflate the central mystery.

**Possible evidence:**

- Hidden colored glass or alchemical powder in the lantern.
- Chemical smell instead of normal oil.
- Bootprints matching local villagers.
- Stolen goods hidden in a shed or cellar.

**Possible checks:**

- Investigation to expose the mechanism.
- Insight to read nervous lies.
- Persuasion or Intimidation to get confessions.
- Non-lethal combat only if players catch thieves in the act.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Some reports are fake, but the thieves copied real behavior seen near the
  forest road.
- Local panic is spreading fast enough for opportunists to profit.
- The real lantern problem is still unresolved.

### Scene 6 - Herbalist's house and diary

**Type:** Core clue scene.

**Purpose:** Provide the clearest forest lead.

**Draft notes:**

- The herbalist is missing.
- His diary should be the strongest direct clue pointing out of the village.
- The house can be quiet and unsettling rather than combat-heavy.
- If players are stuck, make the diary easy to find after one meaningful search
  choice.

**Diary excerpt from campaign draft:**

> Third night of the blue lights. They are not fireflies, not will-o'-wisps,
> and not lanterns carried by human hands. They move as if answering each other.
>
> The village lanterns flickered again tonight, always when the forest lights
> gathered near the old trail.
>
> I will follow at dawn. If I am right, the source is not in the village.

**Possible checks:**

- Investigation to find diary, notes, herb maps, or packed tools.
- Nature/Survival to recognize forest herb routes.
- Arcana to connect the diary's "answering each other" language to broadcast
  behavior.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- The forest lights move in a pattern.
- Village lanterns react when forest lights gather near the old trail.
- The herbalist followed the lights and did not return.
- The source is probably outside the village.

### Scene 7 - Miller's mill and rat problem

**Type:** Optional combat/tutorial side quest.

**Purpose:** Teach initiative, movement, attacks, and simple environmental
clues without replacing the investigation.

**Draft notes:**

- Run only if the group has time or wants a fight.
- The miller would usually ask the hunter for help, but the hunter is missing.
- Abnormal rats can have faint glowing eyes, coordinated behavior, or spiral
  gnaw marks.
- Keep combat short and beginner-friendly.
- Optional reward: old mine lantern that can be recharged with magic later.

**Possible checks and beats:**

- Survival/Investigation behind the mill for strange tracks.
- Short rat fight to teach basic combat.
- Arcana or Perception on the old mine lantern if used as a future darkness
  prep item.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Hunter found strange tracks behind the mill before disappearing.
- Tracks led toward the forest edge.
- The mine lantern can foreshadow darkness and lantern tools in later sessions.

### Scene 8 - Volunteer group trail

**Type:** Flexible clue beat, can attach to tavern, mayor, herbalist, or forest
road.

**Purpose:** Connect Session 1 rumors to the Session 2 mine discovery.

**Draft notes:**

- The previous volunteer group should remain missing during session 1; their
  bodies are discovered near the coal mines in session 2.
- Do not spend a full scene here unless players fixate on them. Treat it as a
  clue layer.

**Possible placements:**

- Bedroll or unpaid tab at the tavern.
- Stablehand saw them heading toward the herbalist's home.
- Alden admits they asked about the hunter.
- Their initials or symbol appear on a post near the forest road.

**Clues to carry forward:**

- Another group followed similar leads and vanished.
- The forest route is dangerous.
- The coal mine/session 2 discovery will feel earned.

### Scene 9 - Mayor follow-up and choice to leave

**Type:** Social decision point.

**Purpose:** Consolidate clues and let the party choose the forest lead.

**Draft notes:**

- Once the party has 2-3 forest-pointing clues, have Alden or the tavern crowd
  react to what they found.
- If they exposed the fake lantern thieves, Alden may try to claim that solves
  the issue. Let players push back with evidence from real lanterns and the
  herbalist diary.
- If they did not expose the fake, no problem. The forest lead still works.
- Give the party a chance to buy supplies, ask the map writer for a route, or
  rest until dawn.

**Minimum clues before ending:**

- Herbalist diary points to forest lights, or
- Miller/hunter tracks point to the woods, or
- Affected homes/real lantern evidence points to the forest road, plus
- Any lead that the volunteer group followed the same trail.

### Scene 10 - Closing image: forest edge

**Type:** Session ending / cliffhanger.

**Purpose:** End with a clear next action and a supernatural image.

**Draft notes:**

- Use once the party commits to the forest, even if not every village node was
  visited.
- Keep the image short. The goal is momentum into Session 2, not another
  investigation loop.

**Suggested closing image from campaign draft:**

> As the party reaches the edge of the trees, the village lanterns behind them
> dim one by one. In the forest ahead, a blue light answers.

## Recommended table order

Use this if running the session as one table night:

1. Scene 0 - framing and contract handout.
2. Scenes 1A-1E - solo intros, in whichever order fits player attention.
3. Scene 2 - tavern convergence.
4. Scene 3 - mayor briefing, if not folded into tavern.
5. Choose 2-3 investigation nodes:
   - affected homes,
   - herbalist's house,
   - fake lantern house,
   - mill if combat tutorial is desired.
6. Scene 9 - clue consolidation and decision.
7. Scene 10 - forest-edge closing image.

## Draft pacing notes

- Five full solo intros can consume close to an hour. Keep each spotlight to one
  main decision and one main roll unless the player is highly engaged.
- The herbalist diary is the clearest mandatory lead. If time is short, make it
  the central investigation node.
- The fake lantern subplot is useful but optional. Run it when the group is
  enjoying deduction or social confrontation.
- The mill is optional and should be used as a rules tutorial, not a required
  clue bottleneck.
- The party does not need every clue. Any 2-3 credible forest clues are enough.
- Mayor Alden should feel evasive and politically weak, not secretly evil.
- Keep "bound souls serving the necromancer" as DM truth for now. Let players
  see symptoms: whispers, breath-pulse, undead sense, cold blue light,
  people walking away from home.

## Open prep notes

- Define 1-2 named affected homes once the village map exists.
- Choose whether the map writer appears in the tavern by default or only if
  players seek maps/records.
- Decide whether to run the mill in session 1 or save combat for the forest.
- Pick a simple stat block for rats if the mill is used.
- Decide whether any named missing villager appears in rumors now and as a

- Confirm whether session 1 starts at level 1 or another level; tune DCs only
