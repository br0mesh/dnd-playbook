### Basic Info

**Character Name:** Harry Potter *(cover identity — see below)*
**Player Name:** ____________________
**Species:** Tiefling
**Class:** Rogue
**Level:** 1
**Background:** Criminal
**Alignment:** Unaligned

**Your real name is Vex.** Only your old crew and anyone hunting the ledger job know it. You have not spoken it aloud in months.

### Personality

Charming but manipulative. You survive through lies, leverage, and secrets, always looking for advantage in every conversation.

### Backstory

You ran with a thieves' string called the **Ashmere lot** in Thieves' Cant — until a **ledger job** went wrong and the whole crew vanished over a fortnight. No bodies, no trials, no word. You got clear. You do not know who took the rest.

Since then you have kept your hood up, coin down, and name buried. Powerful people wanted that ledger buried too. You are headed toward **Briarford** because a contract poster promises fair coin for work vague enough to hide behind — and because a village is a door before the city learns you are still alive.

**Harry Potter** is the false name you will use from Briarford onward. You may choose it in the moment, or carry it in your pocket already — either way, the village gets Harry, not Vex.

### Session 1 Introduction

**Spotlight scene:** *The Wrong Name* — **The Last Lamp**, a squat timber-and-stone tavern on the Briarford road, a league outside the city walls.

Late afternoon. Rain has stopped but the eaves still drip. Sawdust, wet wool, cheap ale. Caravan guards dice in a corner. A clerk from the city assayer's office eats alone, ink on his cuffs.

You sit in a corner booth, back to the wall, hood over your horns. A **contract notice** for Briarford lies on the table — lanterns burning the wrong colour, people walking toward the trees and not coming home, **Mayor Alden** hiring at the village hall. The work is vague. The money is not.

You are reading the pay line and listening to the room. Not drinking heavily. Not bothering anyone. For now, nobody is bothering you.

**What you want from the scene**

- Confirm the Briarford contract is real money, not village panic
- Leave with the poster in your pocket and a route to the village before nightfall
- Learn whether anyone here connects you to the **ledger job**, the **Ashmere lot**, or sealed city records
- Decide what name Briarford will hear — and whether anyone on this road knows **Vex**

**Possible checks**

- **Insight (DC 12)** — read the man at the bar: hunter closing in, or something else? *(Passive Insight 12 counts as success.)*
- **Deception (DC 11)** — bluff your way out: "Wrong tiefling. My name is **Harry Potter**."
- **Stealth (DC 11–12)** — slip out through the crowd, kitchen, or delivery door before the room decides what you are
- **Sleight of Hand (DC 13)** — lift a pouch or folded chit from the speaker at the bar
- **Unarmed strike** *(optional, panic branch only)* — +2 to hit, 1 bludgeoning; goal is escape, not winning a bar fight

**Complication**

After a minute of quiet, a voice at the **bar** cuts through the dice game:

> "...no, I'm telling you — **Vex** was in on the ledger job. Whole crew went sideways after that. Some say the **Ashmere lot** got them. Some say they ran. I haven't seen a soul from that string since winter."

The speaker is **Tomas Grull** — broad-shouldered, one milky eye, city boots dusty from forest roads. He is **not** looking at you yet. He is arguing with the barkeep and a tired caravan guard, half drunk, half boasting.

Then Tomas's good eye **flicks to your corner** — too long to be accidental. He goes quiet. The guard follows his gaze. The room does not explode; it **tightens**. Someone near the door shifts weight.

- On a **failed Insight**, you misread him as a bounty hunter stalling for backup — chest says *run*, and you may not wait to see whether he pulls steel or paper
- On **success**, you read the pause correctly: he recognized you, but he seems **more frightened than hunting**. You can bluff, slip away, follow him outside, or try to rob him before the room decides

**Clues to carry into Briarford** *(leave with at least two)*

- Briarford pays fairly; **Mayor Alden** hires at the **village hall**
- A porch lantern **whispered a name** — Tomas heard it from a frightened carter who fled Briarford last week
- The **Ashmere string** is gone; cause unknown — city money, sealed records, no bodies
- Tomas (or a stolen chit) may tie **ledger copies** to the **Briarford toll-road**
- The speaker wears **city boots** but **forest mud** to the knee

**Intro ending**

> The contract creases in your fist. Mud grabs at your boots where the road turns toward the pines. Behind you, someone may still be shouting **Vex** — or nothing at all. Briarford is hours ahead. Forward feels safer than back. When the village asks your name, you already know the answer: **Harry Potter**.
