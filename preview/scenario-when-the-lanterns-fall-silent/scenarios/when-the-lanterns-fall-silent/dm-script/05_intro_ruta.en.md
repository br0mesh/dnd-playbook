# Intro: Ruta — The Cat and the Blue Light

**Character:** Ruta (half-elf ranger) · **Player spotlight:** ~5–10 minutes · **Teaches:** Animal Handling, Survival, Perception · **Optional:** Stealth, Nature

### Summary

At **early evening**, Ruta camps in a **mossy clearing** off the Briarford forest road — tin plate in hand, supper stolen. A **ginger tom** has dragged her dried fish into a bracken tangle downslope. She is crouched at the fire ring, half amused and half offended, convinced the cat is now her responsibility.

While she negotiates with thorns, a **blue glow** threads through the pines toward Briarford — steady, wrong for oil or hearth-flame. The cat freezes. The light **brightens once**, slow as breath, and the tom **yowls** at the empty air.

**Animal Handling** decides whether Ruta befriends the thief or watches it bolt toward the village. **Survival** and **Perception** place the clearing on the map: city-boot tracks, a contract notice on a downslope marker, and at least one blue porch lantern visible through the trees.

**Ends when:** Ruta leaves the clearing for **Briarford** — at least two clues about the lanterns, a personal stake (coin for the cat sanctuary dream, curiosity, or the ginger tom tagging along), and the village tavern or mayor's hall close enough to ask questions.

**Contract notice (trail marker — handout or paraphrase):**

> **WANTED — BRAVE HANDS**
>
> The village of **Briarford** seeks aid with **unnatural lantern behavior** and **recent disappearances** near homes where porch lights act strangely.
>
> Fair payment upon investigation and resolution.
>
> **Speak with Mayor Alden** at the village hall.
>
> *(City guild stamp in the corner — posting fee paid.)*

**Scene flow:**

1. **Set the scene** — read-aloud arrival; Ruta at the fire ring, cat in the bracken.
2. **Approach the cat** — **Animal Handling** or **Stealth**; reward RP with advantage if the player uses the fish scrap or string from inventory.
3. **Read the forest** — optional **Survival** or **Nature** before the pulse.
4. **Notice the blue light** — **Perception**; passive 15 catches the contract notice without a roll.
5. **Complication** — blue pulse; cat yowls; shadows deepen.
6. **React** — success/failure branches; optional second pulse at DC 13.
7. **Exit** — read contract on marker, descend to **Briarford tavern** or **mayor's hall**.

### Read-aloud

**Arrival**

> The clearing smells of cold ash and pine resin. Your fire is honest but small. Your dinner is not in your hand anymore.
>
> Twenty feet downslope, a **ginger tom** crouches in bracken with a strip of dried fish hanging from his mouth like a victory flag. He is orange, shameless, and clearly prepared to die on this hill.
>
> You have known worse thieves. You have not known worse thieves you immediately wanted to adopt.

**The chase**

> Bracken claws at your sleeves. The cat watches you with the calm of something that has never once doubted its crimes. Behind you, Briarford's ordinary lights wink through the trees — yellow, warm, distant.
>
> Then one light is **not** yellow. **Blue**. Steady. Threading between trunks like someone laid a path in the wrong colour.

**The pulse**

> The blue glow **brightens** — once, slow, like a lung filling.
>
> The ginger tom's ears flatten. He **yowls** at the air between the pines, not at you. For a heartbeat the bracken shadows look too deep, as if the light were looking back.
>
> On the downslope path, a **notice** flutters on a trail marker. Guild stamp. Help wanted.

**Cat friend ending (success)**

> Fish scrap, string, and a humiliating amount of patience win the day. The tom bonks his head against your wrist — once, contractual — and allows himself to be lifted. He is still eating your dinner. You have never been prouder. The blue light breathes again toward the village. Someone there is paying for answers. You start walking.

**Cat chase ending (failed Animal Handling)**

> The tom **bolts** — fish and all — straight toward the downslope path where the blue glow pools between the trees. You can follow a cat, follow the light, or pretend you have dignity. The notice on the trail marker makes the choice easier: **Briarford** wants brave hands. You have two empty hands and a grudge. Good enough.

### Checks

**Animal Handling (DC 11)** — Coax the ginger tom out of the bracken without driving him deeper.

| Result | What happens |
|--------|----------------|
| **Fail (< 11)** | The cat **bolts** toward the downslope path and the blue glow. Ruta can chase (bonus **Athletics DC 12** or **Stealth DC 11**) or let him go. |
| **Success (11–16)** | The tom allows approach; he eats the fish scrap but stays within arm's reach. **Advantage** on the next **Perception** check this scene. |
| **Success by 5+ (17+)** | The cat **follows** Ruta voluntarily — bonks her wrist, sits on her bedroll strap. Named or unnamed, he is now a scene companion until the tavern (flavour only). |

**Passive Animal Handling 13:** Treat as success.

**Stealth (DC 11)** — Optional alternative to Animal Handling: circle the bracken without spooking the cat.

| Result | What happens |
|--------|----------------|
| **Fail** | Snap a twig; cat runs (same as failed Animal Handling). |
| **Success** | Close enough to scoop the cat or grab the fish strip; then **Animal Handling (DC 9)** to keep him calm. |

**Survival (DC 12)** — Read tracks in the clearing before the pulse.

| Result | What Ruta learns |
|--------|------------------|
| **Fail** | Cat tracks everywhere; nothing useful. |
| **Success (12+)** | The tom came from the **village road** today. **City-boot prints** crossed the clearing within the last hour — someone headed downslope in a hurry. |
| **Success by 5+ (17+)** | Boot prints **pause** at the fire ring, then continue toward Briarford without camping. Whoever passed knew the clearing and did not stay. |

**Nature (DC 11)** — Optional alternative to Survival for reading the environment.

| Result | What Ruta learns |
|--------|------------------|
| **Fail** | Ordinary forest edge; squirrels, old ash, nothing ominous. |
| **Success** | No predator kill site — the cat is not fleeing a hunter. The blue light does **not** match foxfire, glow-moss, or any natural source in this biome. |

**Perception (DC 12)** — Study the blue glow and the downslope path.

| Result | What Ruta notices |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Fail (< 12)** | One blue light toward the village; the contract notice is a pale rectangle you might spot on the way out. |
| **Success (12+)** | **One** porch lantern holds blue; ordinary yellow windows flank it. The **contract notice** on the trail marker is legible at this distance: unnatural lanterns, Mayor Alden, fair pay. |
| **Success by 5+ (17+)** | A **second** lantern **flickers** yellow-to-blue and back — out of sync with the first (cross-thread with Lilith / Mordain). The ginger tom stared at that flicker a second before you did. |

**Passive Perception 15:** Treat as success for the contract notice and one blue lantern.

**Perception (DC 13)** — Optional, after the pulse: catch the second out-of-sync lantern or a hooded figure on the downslope path (same optional beat as other intros; do not name unless table order requires).

**Clues to carry into Briarford (leave with at least two):**

- At least one porch lantern burns **blue** while others hold ordinary flame.
- **Contract notice** on the trail marker — Mayor Alden hires for unnatural lantern behavior.
- The ginger tom came from the **village road**; fresh **city-boot prints** crossed the clearing.
- *(Optional)* Second lantern **out of sync** — matches Lilith's and Mordain's hillside notes.
- *(Optional)* Animals react to the pulse before humans — Ruta's ranger angle on the phenomenon.
- *(Optional)* Hooded traveler on the downslope path — cross-thread with Vex or other road intros.

**Arriving in Briarford (all branches):** Ruta reaches the **village tavern** in early evening — hearth lit, locals wary but not hostile. Mayor Alden may be at the hall or sent for when a ranger asks about the blue lights. If **Mordain**, **Lilith**, **Elias**, or **Harry Potter** arrive later, Ruta's story contrasts *"a cat stole my dinner and the light looked back"* with their research, divine pull, and tavern politics.

### Contingencies

- **If Ruta skips all checks:** The pulse and contract notice are still two clues. Run the **Animal Handling** complication anyway — that is the teach moment.
- **If Ruta attacks the cat:** Comedy dies; cat flees; no mechanical penalty. Point toward **Animal Handling** or chase.
- **If Ruta shoots the blue glow:** No target — light is distant. **Perception DC 10** to realize the lantern is village-side, not in the bracken.
- **If Ruta camps until dawn:** Fine — she can still reach the tavern at first light; other PCs may already be inside. Contract remains on the marker overnight.
- **If the player references Lilith's herb ring or Mordain's treeline watch:** Optional — at early evening Lilith may not be at the ring yet; Mordain may already be on the hillside at dusk. Keep intros independent unless the table runs same fictional hour.
- **If Ruta declares the cat sanctuary plan to every NPC:** Locals laugh kindly; Mayor Alden remembers her as *"the cat woman"* — flavour hook only.

### DM Notes

#### The ginger tom (DM only)

Use him for comedy and compass. He is not magical. He **reacts** to the blue pulse one beat before Ruta's passive senses catch it — animals nervous first. If befriended, he tags along to the tavern door and may cause a second comic beat when locals react to a half-elf with a cat. If he bolts, Ruta may find him on the **tavern porch** later eating someone else's fish — no extra roll required.

- **Spotlight rule:** Address the player directly; other PCs are not present. Solo screen time.
- **Teaching moment — Animal Handling:** Calming or directing a **beast** — failure is comedy (cat bolt), not punishment.
- **Teaching moment — Survival / Perception:** Reading **tracks** and **environment** — reward curiosity about the blue light without explaining necromancy yet.
- **Tone:** Kind, curious, lightly comic — mishaps are warm, not cruel. The cat sanctuary dream is sincere; the "five hundred cats" line is character colour.
- **Cross-thread with Lilith:** Same blue pulse phenomenon; Lilith **measures** at midnight, Ruta **feels animals react** at dusk.
- **Cross-thread with Mordain:** Both outdoors at forest edge; Mordain feels divine pull, Ruta sees animal fear first.
- **Cross-thread with Elias:** Scholarly pattern vs ranger instinct — tavern line: *"Your notes say breath. My cat said run."*
- **Cross-thread with Vex:** City-boot prints in the clearing; optional hooded figure on downslope path.
- **Timing:** Ruta can arrive **early evening** while Lilith's intro is after midnight — hand-wave overlap or run Ruta first.
- **Next scene:** Briarford tavern — Mayor Alden, other PCs, contract recognition.
