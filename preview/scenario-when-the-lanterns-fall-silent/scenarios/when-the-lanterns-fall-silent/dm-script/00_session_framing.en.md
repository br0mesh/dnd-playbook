# Session 1 Framing: Contract and Table Order

**Audience:** Whole table · **Before:** Solo intro spotlights · **Teaches:** Shared premise only — no mechanics lesson

### Summary

**Purpose:** Give every player the same contract text, the same village problem, and the same session structure before anyone runs a solo intro. Hide the deeper truth (bound souls, necromancer ritual) — at the table this is a paid job about **unnatural lanterns** and **missing people**.

**Table order tonight:**

1. **This scene (0)** — read contract, explain spotlight order, set the morning appointment.
2. **Solo intros (1A–1E)** — each PC gets 5–10 minutes alone (`01_intro_vex` through `05_intro_ruta`, any order).
3. **Overnight in Briarford** — PCs reach the village on their own roads; sleep at **The Hearth & Horn** or camp nearby. **No group hire briefing yet.**
4. **Morning convergence (`06_mayors_hall_convergence`)** — first light at **Mayor Alden's hall**; Alden present; payment terms and investigation menu.
5. **Village investigation (2–3 nodes)** — affected homes, herbalist, fake lanterns, optional mill — then follow-up and forest edge.

**Ends when:** Every player knows the contract wording, that intros are solo, and that the **full party meets Mayor Alden at the hall at first light** — not at the inn bar tonight.

**Contract poster (handout — identical text in every intro):**

> **WANTED — BRAVE HANDS**
>
> The village of **Briarford** seeks aid with **unnatural lantern behavior** and **recent disappearances** near homes where porch lights act strangely.
>
> Fair payment upon investigation and resolution.
>
> **Speak with Mayor Alden** at the village hall.
>
> *(City guild stamp in the corner — posting fee paid.)*

### Read-aloud

**To the table**

> Before anyone's character walks alone on a road or hillside, you should all hear the same job posting.
>
> **Briarford** is a timber village on the forest edge. For two weeks, porch lanterns have burned the wrong colour, flickered without wind, and — if you believe frightened neighbors — **whispered names**. People have walked toward the trees and not come home. The mayor posted this notice in the city and on every trail marker he could afford.
>
> Pin or pass the poster. The pay line is real. The explanation is not — not yet.

**How tonight runs**

> Next, each of you gets a **short solo scene** — five to ten minutes — on the road or at the forest edge. You are **not** in the same room yet. Teach one skill at a time; keep choices simple.
>
> Every intro ends with your character **heading into Briarford** and finding a bed. The village inn is **The Hearth & Horn**. Warm hearth, wary locals, same contract copied on the **hall board outside** — but **Mayor Alden does not hire the full party tonight**.
>
> When everyone has had a spotlight, **fast-forward to first light**. You all walk to **Mayor Alden's hall** for the morning appointment on the poster. That is when the group forms and the investigation opens.

**What you are not explaining yet**

> Do **not** teach initiative, spell slots, or long rests in this scene. Do **not** reveal that the lanterns are **captured souls** or that a necromancer runs a ritual. At the table, the problem is **fear, rumors, and missing neighbors** — plus fair coin if you solve it.

### Checks

No rolls in Scene 0. If players ask rules questions, point to their intro spotlight or defer to the optional mill fight later.

| Player question | Short answer |
|-----------------|--------------|
| "Do we know each other?" | Your choice — intros do not require prior acquaintance. |
| "What level are we?" | Level 1 unless the table agreed otherwise. |
| "Is the contract a trap?" | Not from Alden's pen — guild stamp is genuine posting fee. |
| "Can we skip intros?" | Only if every player accepts losing personal hooks; not recommended session 1. |

### Contingencies

- **If a player arrives late:** Read the poster to them, assign the intro they missed to run first when they sit down, or pair them with an intro that matches their class.
- **If the table wants one shared opening instead of spotlights:** Run `06_mayors_hall_convergence` cold — but you lose the teaching beats in `01`–`05`. Framing still helps set expectations.
- **If players fixate on the guild stamp:** Note it for `06` Investigation on the hall notice board — do not resolve Ashmere ledger threads here.
- **If someone asks about the previous volunteer group:** "Rumor says they took the job last week. You will hear more at the hall."

### DM Notes

- **Pacing:** 3–5 minutes at the table — poster, order of play, morning appointment. Then jump into intros.
- **Mandatory vs optional:** Scene 0 is **mandatory** for new groups; solo intros are **strongly recommended** but skippable in a pinch.
- **Poster prop:** Print once; reuse in Briarford on the hall door so **Harry Potter**, **Lilith**, and others recognize the same wording.
- **Truth to hide:** Bound souls, necromancer, seal prophecy — symptoms only until later sessions (whispers, breath-pulse, undead sense, cold blue light).
- **Next scene:** Any solo intro (`01`–`05`), then overnight color at **The Hearth & Horn**, then `06_mayors_hall_convergence` at first light.
