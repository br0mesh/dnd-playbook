# Intro: Lilith — Blue Measurements

**Character:** Lilith (elf wizard) · **Player spotlight:** ~5–10 minutes · **Teaches:** Arcana or Investigation, herb gathering

### Summary

Well after midnight, Lilith works at the **forest edge above Briarford** — a ring of faintly luminescent herbs that only glow under the village lanterns' blue light. A crystal focus hovers before her, turning slowly while she records readings in a cramped notebook. The nearest porch lantern on the far side of the village pulses in a rhythm that does not match wind, weather, or any natural source she has catalogued at the academy.

She is not afraid yet. She is fascinated.

Before dawn bleaches the samples, Lilith can **gather starwort** from the ring and **interpret the lantern's magical signature**. When a porch lantern flares and a whisper shaped like a name rides the pulse, an **Arcana** check decides whether she misreads the event as harmless resonance or acts in time to protect her focus and her notes.

**Ends when:** Lilith leaves the hillside with at least one herb sample, at least two clues about Briarford's lanterns, and a reason to enter the village — usually the **Briarford tavern** at first light, where the contract is posted and other hirelings may already be gathering.

**Contract notice (trail marker — handout or paraphrase):**

> **WANTED — BRAVE HANDS**
>
> The village of **Briarford** seeks aid with **unnatural lantern behavior** and **recent disappearances** near homes where porch lights act strangely.
>
> Fair payment upon investigation and resolution.
>
> **Speak with Mayor Alden** at the village hall.
>
> *(City guild stamp in the corner — posting fee paid.)*

**Scene flow:**

1. **Set the scene** — read-aloud arrival; Lilith is measuring ambient magic and the herb ring.
2. **Gather herbs** (optional but encouraged) — **Investigation** or careful RP; see Checks.
3. **Observe the lantern** — **Arcana** or **Investigation** to interpret the pattern before the flare.
4. **Complication** — porch lantern flares; name-whisper; focus cracks.
5. **React** — **Arcana (DC 12)** on the flare; success branches vs failure.
6. **Exit** — pack samples, pick up contract from trail marker, descend to **Briarford tavern** at first light.

### Read-aloud

**Arrival**

> Briarford sleeps below you — rooflines, chimney smoke, ordinary yellow windows — except for one porch light on the far side that burns **blue**. Up here at the treeline, the air feels charged, like the moment before a storm that never breaks.
>
> You kneel inside a ring of low herbs. They do not glow by moonlight alone. When the blue lantern pulses, the leaves answer with a thin silver-green light, as if the village were watering them from a distance.
>
> Your crystal focus hangs in the air, turning. The notebook on your knee is already half full of numbers that should not repeat this cleanly in nature.

**The ring**

> Three species in the ring, maybe four. Two you recognize from academy texts — common starwort and night-cress. The third only luminesces when the blue light spikes. That one is new. That one is why you are still awake.

**The flare**

> The blue porch lantern **brightens** — one heartbeat, two — and something rides the pulse. Not speech. Shaped like a **name** whispered through glass.
>
> Your focus **cracks**. A hairline of blue light runs through the crystal like frost. The starwort ring flares in sympathy and then dims, as if holding its breath.

**Quiet ending (success)**

> You wrap the samples in oiled cloth before the light can steal them. The cracked focus still hums — wrong, but usable. Whatever Briarford is broadcasting, it is worth more than a notebook page. You follow the road downhill toward the first warm window.

**Rushed ending (failed Arcana)**

> You tell yourself it is only resonance — academy theory says fields this size should dissipate. You stay in the open ring one beat too long. The focus shivers; ink smears in your notebook where the pulse hit. You grab what samples you can and move downslope before the next flare finds you.

### Checks

#### Gathering herbs

Lilith's immediate goal is a **starwort sample** that keeps its properties after dawn. The ring holds enough material for several doses if harvested carefully.

**Investigation (DC 12)** — cut the right stems, time the harvest to a blue pulse, and preserve sap in vials from your scholar's pack.

| Result | What Lilith gets |
|--------|------------------|
| **Fail (< 12)** | **1 dose** — crushed leaves; luminescence fades in **1 hour** unless kept near blue lantern light |
| **Success (12–16)** | **2 doses** — stable until **dawn** if wrapped in oiled cloth |
| **Success by 5+ (17+)** | **3 doses** — one includes the **anomalous third species**; stable for **24 hours**; reacts visibly to detect_magic_1st_divination within 30 ft/6 tiles of active blue lantern light |

**Arcana (DC 13)** — alternative harvest method: pull samples when the magical background dips between pulses (no Investigation proficiency required, but higher DC).

| Result | What Lilith gets |
|--------|------------------|
| **Fail (< 13)** | **1 dose** — starwort only; properties fade at dawn |
| **Success (13+)** | **2 doses** — includes notes on which pulse phase preserves the anomaly |

**No roll:** If the player describes a careful, methodical harvest (vials ready, timing with the pulse, no rushing), grant **1 dose** without a check — reward good RP.

**Nature or Survival (DC 12):** Allow if the player asks to use either skill and has proficiency. Same success tiers as Investigation.

**Time cost:** Each harvest attempt takes about **10 minutes** in-world. Lilith can attempt **once** before the flare complication unless she skips observing the lantern to rush a second try (then run the flare without prior Arcana data — harder, see below).

**What the herbs are (share on success):**

- **Blue-lantern starwort** — common reagent; luminesces only under Briarford's blue porch light, not ordinary flame.
- The **anomalous third species** (high Investigation/Arcana success only) — unknown name; your notebook label: *"Briarford pulse-weed?"* — spikes detect magic readings toward the village, not away from it.

**Arcana (DC 11)** — Before the flare: interpret the lantern's magical signature and compare it to academy theory.

| Result | What Lilith learns |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Fail (< 11)** | The pulse **feels** natural — ley-line echo, weather charm, or residual enchantment. You have no warning when the flare hits. |
| **Success (11+)** | The signature is **directed** — broadcast toward the village, not ambient. You may **mark the pulse rhythm** in your notebook (advantage on the flare Arcana check). |
| **Success by 5+ (16+)** | You recognize a **name-shaped carrier wave** in the pattern — the next pulse will carry speech-like information. You can prepare **shield_1st_abjuration** or cover the focus as a free action when the flare comes. |

**Passive Arcana 11:** Treat as success for pre-flare observation.

**Investigation (DC 12)** — Before the flare: trace whether the anomaly lives in the herbs, soil, or air.

| Result | What Lilith learns |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Fail (< 12)** | The ring reacts to the lantern, but you cannot tell **where** the effect originates. |
| **Success (12+)** | The **soil and air** carry the same frequency as the herbs — the village is the source, not the hillside. |
| **Success by 5+ (17+)** | Faint **foot traffic** on the downslope path — someone passed toward the village within the last hour. |

**Arcana (DC 12)** — When the flare hits: read the pulse and protect your work.

| Result | What Lilith believes | What is actually true (DM only) |
|--------|----------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Fail (< 12)** | Harmless **resonance** — stay put, keep measuring. | The flare is a **directed echo** from the lantern network; staying exposed risks another crack in focus or spoiled samples. |
| **Success (12+)** | The pulse carries **information**, not just energy — shield the focus, move to cover, or record the pattern. | Same truth — the whisper is a **name-carrier** tied to Briarford's porch lanterns; not yet decipherable without village context. |

**Passive Arcana 12:** Treat as success on the flare check.

**Perception (DC 13)** — Optional, any time before leaving: movement in the treeline, a second blue light, or a watcher on the road.

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Fail (< 13)** | Nothing clear — wind, owls, distant dog. |
| **Success (13+)** | A **second blue lantern** flickers on a house nearer the forest road — out of sync with the first. |
| **Success by 5+ (18+)** | Someone on the **downslope path** — hooded, city boots, pausing when the lantern flares (could be Vex or another PC route; do not name them unless you know the table order). |

#### Branch A — Failed Arcana on the flare

Lilith misreads the pulse and stays in the open ring.

- Focus takes a **second hairline crack** — still functional; cosmetic until repaired.
- Any herb doses not wrapped in oiled cloth **fade one tier sooner** (dawn instead of 24 hours, etc.).
- She can still **Investigation (DC 10)** to salvage notes and samples before leaving.
- **Exit:** Pack what remains, take the contract from the trail marker, descend to the tavern — shaken but loaded with questions.

#### Branch B — Success on the flare

Lilith acts in time. Player chooses one:

**Option 1 — Shield the focus**

- Cast **shield_1st_abjuration** or cover the focus with lead-lined cloth from your pack (no slot if already cast; otherwise spend slot or describe physical cover — either works).
- Focus stays ** intact**; notebook pages remain readable.
- Record the **pulse interval** — useful in the tavern if another PC mentions whispered names.

**Option 2 — Record the pattern**

- Advantage on **Investigation (DC 12)** to capture the name-shaped waveform in notation.
- Success: Lilith has a **sketch of the carrier wave** — Mayor Alden or a tavern witness may confirm villagers hear names on their porches.
- Failure: partial sketch; still counts as one clue.

**Option 3 — Test the herbs**

- Hold a starwort sample toward the flare. **Arcana (DC 10)**.
- Success: the anomalous species **brightens in sync** — proof the village broadcast and the ring are linked.
- Failure: sample wilts; lose **1 dose** but learn the pulse has a **harmful overtone** for living reagents.

**Option 4 — Follow the second light**

- Requires prior **Perception** success (second lantern). Lilith packs samples and **Stealth (DC 12)** or walks openly toward the nearer house.
- Success: sees a porch lantern burning blue with **no one home** — door ajar, cold hearth. Contract-style chalk mark on the step: *"Ask the mayor."*
- Failure: a neighbor shutter slams; Lilith retreats to the tavern without a fight.

**Clues to carry into Briarford (leave with at least two):**

- Herbs only luminesce under **Briarford blue lantern light**, not ordinary flame.
- Magical background **spikes toward the village**, not away from it.
- A porch lantern **whispered a name-shaped pulse** during the flare.
- **Contract notice** on the trail marker — Mayor Alden hires at the **village hall**.
- *(Optional)* Second blue lantern **out of sync** with the first.
- *(Optional)* **Pulse-weed** sample reacts to detect magic near active blue lanterns.

**Arriving in Briarford (all branches):** Lilith enters the **village tavern** at first light — warm hearth, nervous locals, contract posted or copied from the trail marker. Mayor Alden may be present or sent for. She introduces herself as a field scholar; starwort samples stay wrapped until she chooses to share them.

### Contingencies

- **If Lilith skips herb gathering:** She still has notebook readings and the contract hook. Grant one clue from Arcana on the flare only — she arrives with knowledge but no physical reagents.
- **If Lilith casts detect_magic_1st_divination:** The blue lantern reads as **evocation** with a faint **necromancy** undertone (DM only — do not label necromancy aloud unless she succeeds **Arcana DC 14**). The herb ring reads as **transmutation** while the blue light is active.
- **If Lilith casts find_familiar_1st_conjuration before the scene:** Familiar can **Help** on one harvest or Perception check; still one harvest attempt before the flare unless she delays the complication by choice.
- **If the player wants combat:** No fight required. Perception success might hint at a watcher; do not force an encounter. Session 1 convergence assumes Lilith reaches the tavern.
- **If Arcana fails twice:** Focus cracked but usable; herbs partially spoiled; she still reaches the tavern with the contract and one strong clue — failure teaches the rule without blocking the session.
- **If Lilith tries to enter a home at night:** Neighbor suspicion only — no burglary needed. Point her to the tavern and mayor.

### DM Notes

- **Spotlight rule:** Address the player directly; other PCs are not present. Solo screen time.
- **Teaching moment:** **Arcana** = magical cause and meaning; **Investigation** = physical trace and careful procedure. Either can be the "main" teach — offer both for herb work vs lantern reading.
- **Herb rewards:** Doses are **story reagents**, not combat loot. They can justify detect magic readings in the tavern, impress an herbalist NPC later, or echo session 3 shrine herbs — do not require item cards unless you add them to the scenario stash later.
- **Cross-thread with Vex:** The name-whisper matches what Tomas heard on the road. Lilith **experiences** the phenomenon; Vex **hears about** it.
- **Cross-thread with Elias:** Same impossible blue. Elias compares accounts; Lilith measures one.
- **Next scene:** Briarford tavern convergence — Mayor Alden, other PCs, contract recognition.
- **Poster prop:** Same contract text as `01_intro_vex.en.md` so players recognize it.
