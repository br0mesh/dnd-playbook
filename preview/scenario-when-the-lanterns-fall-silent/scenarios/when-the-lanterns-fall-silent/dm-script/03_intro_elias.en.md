# Intro: Elias — The Same Colour Twice

**Character:** Elias Vale (half-elf sorcerer) · **Player spotlight:** ~5–10 minutes · **Teaches:** Arcana and History, optional Insight / Persuasion

### Summary

Late evening at **The Blue Wick** — a narrow timber inn on the Briarford road, a few hours shy of the village, where wagon traffic thins and the pines press close to the windows. A contract poster for Briarford hangs near the hearth. Most travelers are asleep or drunk.

Elias sits at a corner table with half-finished notes and a cold cup of tea. He is not reading the pay rate. He is comparing three copied accounts of **blue porch lights** — a caravan guard's hand, a frightened farmer's scrawl, and a half-legible journal page bought for two copper. When the hanging lantern outside the window flickers the wrong colour, his fingers twitch toward a cantrip he has not cast yet tonight.

A travelling peddler insists the lights are **dragon omens** and tries to sell a cheap bronze trinket. When Elias scoffs, the peddler's pack lantern gutters — and for one heartbeat the flame turns the same impossible blue. **History** or **Arcana** checks on the accounts decide what lore Elias connects; **Arcana** on the flare decides whether he recognizes the colour as the same one that awakened his power in the wilderness.

**Ends when:** Elias leaves for **Briarford** before dawn with at least two clues about the lanterns, his notes reconciled enough to trust the contract, and a personal stake — research, reckoning, or both — to carry into the village tavern where **Mayor Alden** and other hirelings may already be gathering.

**Contract poster (hearth — handout or paraphrase):**

> **WANTED — BRAVE HANDS**
>
> The village of **Briarford** seeks aid with **unnatural lantern behavior** and **recent disappearances** near homes where porch lights act strangely.
>
> Fair payment upon investigation and resolution.
>
> **Speak with Mayor Alden** at the village hall.
>
> *(City guild stamp in the corner — posting fee paid.)*

**Scene flow:**

1. **Set the scene** — read-aloud arrival; Elias compares three accounts at the corner table.
2. **Reconcile the accounts** — **History** or **Arcana**; see Checks.
3. **Draw out the room** (optional) — **Insight**, **Persuasion**, or **Deception** on other travelers.
4. **The flicker** — window lantern; personal beat; optional **Arcana** before the peddler.
5. **Complication** — peddler and pack-lantern blue flare.
6. **React** — **Arcana (DC 12)** on the flare; success branches vs failure.
7. **Exit** — gather papers, head for **Briarford tavern** before dawn.

### Read-aloud

**Arrival**

> The Blue Wick smells of stale tea and wet wool. Outside, the Briarford road is dark; the village itself is still hours ahead. Most of the common room has turned in — one snoring merchant, a dying fire, rain ticking the shutters.
>
> Your corner table is a mess of purpose. Three copied witness accounts. A journal page with the ink washed pale in one corner. A contract poster on the hearth you have already memorised without meaning to.
>
> You are not here for coin. You are here because every description of Briarford's porch lights uses the same word your wilderness notes refuse to spell correctly: **blue** — the wrong blue, the one that answered you once in open country before you knew what you were.

**The accounts**

> The guard writes like a soldier: *"Lights pulse like breath. Not wind. Door left open. Man walked toward trees."*
>
> The farmer's hand shakes through the copy: *"It whispered. Not words — shaped like a name. We barred the door."*
>
> The journal page is worse — half bought, half stolen: *"...posting fee paid at mayor's hall... guild seal... dead light in the old songs..."*
>
> None of them agree on the cause. All of them agree on the colour.

**The flicker**

> The hanging lantern outside the window **stutters**. Yellow. Yellow. Then — for one heartbeat — **blue**, clean and impossible, like a colour that forgot it was pretending to be flame.
>
> Your fingers move before your mind finishes the thought. A cantrip sits behind your teeth. The power in your blood **recognises** something. You do not cast yet. The room has not noticed.

**The peddler**

> A travelling peddler slides into the chair opposite without asking. Pack lantern, bronze trinkets, a grin that has sold worse lies in better towns.
>
> "Scholar, yes? I can tell. You're chasing the Briarford lights." He taps a cheap bronze disk on the table. "Dragon omens, friend. Bronze scales, bronze sky. You want protection, you want the real story — ten copper."
>
> You scoff. Maybe you say *"I'd like to formally object to the concept of cursed villages."* Maybe you just look at him.
>
> His pack lantern **gutters**. The flame turns the same impossible **blue** as the window light — one heartbeat — then coughs back to yellow. The peddler goes white.

**Quiet ending (success)**

> The tea is still cold, but your notes finally agree on one thing: Briarford is not selling a ghost story. You wrap the journal page, tuck the peddler's trinket into your pack — evidence, not superstition — and leave before dawn. Half hoping the lights will answer you. Half hoping they will not. The village tavern is the next warm window.

**Dismissive ending (failed Arcana on the flare)**

> Smoke and cheap oil, you tell yourself. Parlor trick. The peddler flees before you can press him, and the window lantern is ordinary yellow again — but your fingers still hum, and the wilderness memory will not stay buried. You go to Briarford anyway. Research does not require certainty. It requires witnesses.

### Checks

**History (DC 11)** — Connect the journal page and witness accounts to regional lore, guild practice, or academy archives.

| Result | What Elias learns |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Fail (< 11)** | Old superstition and bad weather. The *"dead light"* phrase on the journal page is probably local slang. |
| **Success (11+)** | **City guild posting fees** match the seal on the hearth contract — Briarford paid to broadcast this hire widely. The journal's *"dead light in the old songs"* echoes **pre-village ballads** about lanterns going quiet before a seal failed. |
| **Success by 5+ (16+)** | You recall an academy archive entry: **circles of mages** once worked rituals at sites near this forest. Solo casters cannot sustain that scale today. Briarford's lights behave like **directed work**, not folk curse. |

**Passive History 13:** Treat as success.

**Arcana (DC 12)** — Compare the three witness descriptions to Elias's own wilderness notes (emission profile, pulse rhythm, response to speech).

| Result | What Elias learns |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Fail (< 12)** | Similar colour, insufficient data. Could be unrelated phenomena sharing a hue. |
| **Success (12+)** | Same **signature**: breath-like pulse, wrong colour temperature, reaction to **names or fear** — not wind or oil variance. Briarford matches the wilderness encounter. |
| **Success by 5+ (17+)** | The pattern is **broadcast**, not ambient — multiple lanterns answering each other. You can **predict the next pulse** (advantage on the flare Arcana check). |

**Passive Arcana 13:** Treat as success.

**Either check counts** toward reconciling the accounts. If Elias succeeds on **both**, grant the higher-tier insight from each table.

**Arcana (DC 11)** — When the window lantern flickers, before the peddler sits down.

| Result | What happens |
|--------|--------------|
| **Fail (< 11)** | You feel the power stir but cannot place why. The peddler's arrival breaks your concentration. |
| **Success (11+)** | The flicker matches your wilderness notes exactly. Your bloodline **warms** — bronze dragon ancestry tingles, but the light is **not** draconic flame. Something else wearing a familiar hue. |
| **Success by 5+ (16+)** | The light **leans toward you** a fraction — not movement, attention. You may **whisper your name** as a free test; the window lantern brightens once on a success here (personal connection confirmed — see Branch B, Option 2). |

**Insight (DC 12)** — Optional, on the peddler or the snoring merchant waking in the corner.

| Result | What Elias reads |
|--------|------------------|
| **Fail (< 12)** | Peddler is a scam artist; merchant is drunk. |
| **Success (12+)** | Peddler is **scared**, not performing — he has seen the blue light before and believes his own pitch. |
| **Success by 5+ (17+)** | Merchant mutters in sleep about **"the name on the porch"** — third-hand Briarford rumor without knowing it. |

**Persuasion (DC 11)** or **Deception (DC 13)** — Draw out witnesses without revealing how deeply the blue light matters to Elias.

| Result | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Fail** | Room stays quiet; you rely on your copied accounts only. |
| **Success** | The **caravan guard** (if still downstairs) confirms: *"Pulse like breath. Man walked toward trees."* Matches account #1. |
| **Success by 5+** | Guard adds: heard from a **city-road tavern** last week that a porch light **whispered a name** — same rumour Tomas may have told Vex at The Last Lamp. |

**Arcana (DC 12)** — When the peddler's pack lantern flares blue.

| Result | What Elias believes | What is actually true (DM only) |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Fail (< 12)** | Cheap trick — phosphorus, tinted glass, or timed oil. | The flare is a **sympathetic echo** of the same broadcast affecting Briarford's lanterns; the peddler's fear triggered a weak resonance in his pack light. |
| **Success (12+)** | Same emission as the window flicker and your wilderness encounter — not stagecraft. | Same truth — **evocation** with a faint **necromancy** undertone (do not label necromancy aloud unless Elias succeeds **Arcana DC 14** or casts detect_magic_1st_divination). |

**Passive Arcana 13:** Treat as success on the flare check.

#### Branch A — Failed Arcana on the flare

Elias dismisses the peddler's lantern as a trick.

- Peddler **bolts** for the road (no chase required).
- Elias keeps the **journal page** and **witness copies** — still enough to justify Briarford.
- **Personal connection** stays subconscious — fingers hum, but he has no proof the light answered him.
- **History success** from earlier still counts as a clue.
- **Exit:** Pack up, walk to Briarford before dawn — skeptical tone, research framing.

#### Branch B — Success on the flare

Elias reads the flare correctly. Player chooses one:

**Option 1 — Record the pattern**

- **Investigation (DC 12)** with advantage if prior Arcana succeeded by 5+.
- Success: notation of **pulse interval and hue shift** — Lilith's later measurements may match.
- Failure: partial notes; still one clue.

**Option 2 — Test personal connection**

- Step to the window or speak your **name** quietly toward the hanging lantern.
- **Arcana (DC 10)** — success: lantern **brightens once** in answer; sorcerous power **stirs** (no slot spent; flavour only).
- Failure: no visible answer — Elias leaves with scientific doubt but emotional certainty.

**Option 3 — Press the peddler**

- **Intimidation (DC 11)** or **Persuasion (DC 13)** before he flees.
- Success: peddler admits he saw blue fire **three nights ago** on the road from Briarford; he sells trinkets because people are frightened.
- Failure: peddler runs; Elias can **Investigation (DC 10)** on the dropped trinket for the guild mark.

**Option 4 — Follow outside**

- Track the peddler into the yard. **Stealth (DC 11)** or walk openly.
- Success: peddler vomits behind the stable, muttering *"dragon omens"* — then his pack lantern flickers blue again, weaker, **toward the forest road to Briarford**.
- Failure: peddler gone; window lantern still worth examining (Option 1 or 2).

**The bronze trinket (any branch where Elias keeps it):**

- **History (DC 10):** Maker's mark from a **city assayer's guild** — same stamp family as the contract posting fee.
- **Arcana (DC 12):** Trinket has **no magic**; the peddler's pitch about bronze dragons is wrong — but Elias's **bronze bloodline** still reacted to the colour, not the metal.

**Clues to carry into Briarford (leave with at least two):**

- Witnesses describe blue light that **pulses like breath**, not wind.
- Journal page mentions **Mayor Alden** and **guild-paid posting** at the village hall.
- Peddler's trinket bears a **city guild maker's mark** from academy archives.
- *(Optional)* Caravan guard confirms **name-shaped whispers** — matches Vex's road rumour and Lilith's measurement.
- *(Optional)* Window or pack lantern **answered Elias's name** — personal reckoning, not just research.
- *(Optional)* *"Dead light"* in old songs — **History** hook toward seal lore (campaign part 2).

**Arriving in Briarford (all branches):** Elias reaches the **village tavern** at first light — contract on the wall, nervous locals, Mayor Alden present or sent for. He introduces himself as a scholar; wilderness notes stay private until he trusts the table. If **Lilith** is already there with wrapped herb samples, the word **blue** is enough to start a conversation.

### Contingencies

- **If Elias skips social checks:** The three written accounts plus contract poster are enough for two clues. Run the peddler complication anyway — that is the teach moment.
- **If Elias casts detect_magic_1st_divination:** Active blue light reads **evocation**; faint **necromancy** undertone only on **Arcana DC 14**. The peddler's trinket is nonmagical.
- **If Elias casts light_cantrip_evocation on his notes:** Normal cantrip light — no blue. Contrast reinforces that Briarford's emission is wrong.
- **If Elias attacks the peddler:** Innkeeper **Corwin** (human, tired, has a club) demands restitution and ejects him. No murder needed — peddler has **AC 11**, **4 HP**. Scene still ends on the road to Briarford.
- **If Elias casts fire_bolt_cantrip_evocation at the lantern:** Misses or shatters a harmless oil lamp; blue flicker **does not return** tonight. Personal test moves to Briarford proper — do not punish curiosity harshly.
- **If History and Arcana both fail:** Elias still has the journal page and contract. He goes to Briarford because the accounts **feel** true — failure teaches that lore and analysis have limits, not that the hook is false.
- **If the player references The Last Lamp:** Optional flavour — a guard mentions *"some tiefling trouble near the city yesterday"* without naming Vex/Harry Potter unless that intro already ran.

### DM Notes

#### Wilderness memory (DM only — share in pieces)

Months ago, on a research trip away from the academy, Elias camped in open country. A **blue light** moved at the treeline — not will-o'-wisp behaviour, not torchlight. It **pulsed like breath**. When he spoke his own name aloud (testing a half-remembered lecture), the light **brightened once**, and sorcerous power **woke** in his blood the next morning.

He has never told anyone the full sequence. The Briarford accounts are the first time strangers describe the same emission profile.

- **Spotlight rule:** Address the player directly; other PCs are not present. Solo screen time.
- **Teaching moment:** **History** = context, archives, and what the region *remembers*; **Arcana** = emission profile, magical cause, and whether the light **recognises** Elias. Offer both before the peddler forces a decision.
- **Personal blue-light thread:** Elias's power awoke after the wilderness encounter. This scene asks whether Briarford repeats it. Success on personal connection (Option 2) is **flavour**, not a new class feature — but it should feel meaningful at the table.
- **Bronze dragon bloodline:** Peddler's *"dragon omens"* pitch is **misdirection**. Elias may joke about it; the real phenomenon is older and uglier (bound-soul lanterns — reveal later).
- **Cross-thread with Vex:** Name-whisper rumour on the road. Elias **reads about** it; Vex **heard** it from Tomas; Lilith **measures** it.
- **Cross-thread with Lilith:** Same impossible blue. Elias compares accounts; Lilith measures one. If both succeed on notation, their notebooks match.
- **Cross-thread with Mordain:** Divine pull vs scholarly pattern — tavern convergence can contrast *"the light chose me"* vs *"the light matches my notes."*
