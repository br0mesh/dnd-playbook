# Intro: Mordain — Before the Threshold

**Character:** Mordain (half-orc paladin) · **Player spotlight:** ~5–10 minutes · **Teaches:** Insight, Divine Sense · **Optional:** Perception, Religion, Survival

### Summary

At **dusk**, Mordain stands alone at the **forest edge above Briarford** — chain mail under a traveler's cloak, shield on his back, no temple behind him and no oath spoken aloud. Something **chose him on the road months ago**: warmth behind the ribs, direction without words. He has followed it ever since, never quickly, never lightly.

From the treeline the village looks ordinary — smoke, rooflines, the last orange of sunset — until a porch lantern on the far side flares the **wrong colour**. Blue. Steady. Not wind. Not hearth-flame.

The weight in his chest turns toward it like a compass finding north. Rumors of Briarford's lanterns felt like the same signal. Not a choice. A pull.

When the blue lantern **pulses once** — slow, like a breath — the force inside Mordain answers. His holy symbol grows warm against his sternum. For a heartbeat he knows exactly which path down the hill to take, whether he wants to or not.

An **Insight** check decides whether he reads that sensation correctly — guidance, warning, or something wearing the shape of both — or misreads it as **certainty** and commits before he has watched the village long enough. On a clear read, he may **test the pull**, descend openly, or spend his **Divine Sense** to learn whether anything unholy is tied to the light.

**Ends when:** Mordain leaves the treeline for **Briarford** — at least one clue about the lanterns, a personal stake in what chose him, and the village tavern (or mayor's hall) close enough to ask questions before the lanterns change again.

**Contract notice (village hall board — visible from the hillside):**

> **WANTED — BRAVE HANDS**
>
> The village of **Briarford** seeks aid with **unnatural lantern behavior** and **recent disappearances** near homes where porch lights act strangely.
>
> Fair payment upon investigation and resolution.
>
> **Speak with Mayor Alden** at the village hall.
>
> *(City guild stamp in the corner — posting fee paid.)*

**Scene flow:**

1. **Set the scene** — read-aloud arrival; Mordain watches Briarford from the treeline at dusk.
2. **Survey the village** (optional) — **Perception**, **Religion**, or **Survival** before the pulse.
3. **Feel the pull** — roleplay the ease/tighten beat when facing downhill vs forest; no roll required.
4. **Complication** — blue lantern pulses; holy symbol warms; path-knowledge hits.
5. **Read the sensation** — **Insight (DC 12)**; failure vs success branches.
6. **React** — on success, choose test / open descent / **Divine Sense**; on failure, commit early.
7. **Exit** — descend to **Briarford tavern** or **mayor's hall**; contract in sight.

### Read-aloud

**Arrival**

> Briarford lies below the treeline — chimney smoke, patched roofs, dogs barking somewhere near the mill. Sunset paints the windows gold. For a moment it could be any village at the edge of any forest.
>
> Then you see the porch lantern on the far side of town. **Blue**. Steady. Not wind. Not hearth-flame.
>
> The thing in your chest — the warmth that chose you on the road months ago, the direction you never asked for — **turns toward it**. No one has spoken your name. No contract has been pressed into your hand. Your body already knows you are going down the hill. You have not moved yet. That is the only part still yours.

**The watch**

> You stand where the pines thin and the path begins its descent. Chain mail hidden under a traveler's cloak. Shield strapped to your back. Holy symbol cold against your sternum — for now.
>
> Most lanterns hold ordinary flame. One does not. On the village hall board, even from here, you can make out a **guild-stamped notice** fluttering in the evening breeze. Help wanted. Unnatural lantern behavior.
>
> When you face downhill, the pull **eases**, like a rope going slack. When you turn back to the forest, it **tightens** — not pain, not quite. Insistence.

**The pulse**

> The blue lantern **brightens once** — slow, like a breath held and released.
>
> Your holy symbol **warms** against your chest. Heat spreads through the mail. For one heartbeat you **know** the path: left of the split oak, past the stone way-marker, down to the hall where the notice hangs.
>
> The knowledge is perfect. That is what frightens you.

**Certainty ending (failed Insight)**

> Perfection feels like permission. You do not wait for a second pulse. You do not ask whether the warmth is mercy or leash. Boots find the path. The forest releases you. Briarford gets closer with every step — and you still do not know what chose you.

**Measured ending (success)**

> The warmth fades to ember-heat, not command. You have watched long enough to choose **how** you go down — openly, testing, or with your god-sense raised like a lantern of your own. The blue light does not dim. The village is real. The road is clear.

### Checks

**Perception (DC 12)** — Optional, before the pulse: count lanterns, movement, watchers.

| Result | What Mordain notices |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Fail (< 12)** | One blue light; the rest look normal from this distance. |
| **Success (12+)** | **One** porch lantern holds blue; a second window **flickers** yellow-to-blue and back — out of sync with the first. |
| **Success by 5+ (17+)** | Movement on the **downslope path** — hooded figure, city boots, pausing when the blue lantern brightens (could be Vex or another PC route; do not name unless you know table order). |

**Passive Perception 12:** Treat as success for "one blue lantern."

**Religion (DC 11)** or **Survival (DC 12)** — Optional, before the pulse: read whether the light fits consecrated ground, old regional faith, or something that does not belong to any shrine you know.

| Result | What Mordain believes | What is actually true (DM only) |
|--------|----------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Fail** | Folk charm, bad oil, or weather witchery — unsettling, not holy. | The light is **necromantic broadcast** wearing a familiar hue; no regional saint claims it. |
| **Success (Religion 11+ / Survival 12+)** | The blue does **not** match temple flame or sanctioned feast-day lights. Old **way-shrine** songs describe porch fires "going quiet" before a seal failed — Briarford may sit on forgotten ground. | **Dead light** folklore points at the old seal; lanterns are **soul-bound**. |
| **Success by 5+** | The forest path below lacks **consecrated** markers; the village side feels **claimed** by something that is not your god — or not only your god. | Desecrated influence on the approach road; divine pull and unholy lanterns **both** point at Briarford. |

**Insight (DC 12)** — When the pulse hits and the holy symbol warms: read the **sensation**, not the village facts.

| Result | What Mordain believes | What is actually true (DM only) |
|--------|----------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Fail (< 12)** | The warmth is **certainty** — holy approval. Waiting is disobedience. | The pull is **urgent** but not infallible; the pulse also carries a **warning** undertone (undead network waking). |
| **Success (12+)** | The sensation holds **two shapes**: **guidance** toward Briarford and **warning** — something unholy is tied to the same light. You can still choose how to obey. | Same truth — divine attention and necromantic broadcast **overlap** at Briarford; Insight success lets Mordain feel both without naming either. |
| **Success by 5+ (17+)** | The warning is sharper: the warmth spiked when the blue lantern pulsed, not when you faced the village. Whatever chose you is reacting to **that light**, not to your fear. | The bound-soul flare triggered a divine **counter-signal** in Mordain's symbol. |

**Passive Insight 14:** Treat as success.

#### Branch A — Failed Insight (certainty)

Mordain misreads the pulse as permission and **commits before a second watch**.

- He takes the **known path** immediately (left of split oak, past way-marker) without optional Perception / Religion.
- **Perception (DC 14)** only if the player asks to look around while moving — success: glimpse the **second flickering lantern**; failure: miss it.
- He may still use **Divine Sense** on the way down, but describe it as **instinct** fighting **momentum** — no penalty, but roleplay the rush.
- **Exit:** Reaches the **tavern door** at full dusk; contract on the hall board is old news by the time he walks past. Personal stake = *"I obeyed before I understood."*

#### Branch B — Success (choose your descent)

You have a beat to act before the next pulse.

**Option 1 — Test the pull**

- Take **ten steps along the wrong path** — upslope or east along the treeline, away from the split oak.
- The warmth **tightens**; holy symbol stays hot until you turn back toward Briarford.
- **Insight (DC 10)** optional: success confirms the pull is **directional**, not general anxiety; failure leaves doubt but no mechanical penalty.
- Then descend on your terms. Grant **advantage on first social check** in the tavern if Mordain can say he did not run blind.

**Option 2 — Watch one more pulse**

- Hold position until the blue lantern breathes again (~1 minute in-world).
- **Perception (DC 11):** success — pulse interval matches **human breath**, not wind; second lantern flickers **one beat later**.
- **Religion (DC 10):** success — old way-shrine verse: *"Dead light sees the road."*
- Then descend. Extra clue without spending Divine Sense.

**Option 3 — Divine Sense (teach moment)**

Mordain invokes his paladin sense before descending past the way-marker — **bonus action**, **1 use per long rest**, lasts until end of his **next turn**.

Explain to the player:

> **Divine Sense** lets you detect **celestials, fiends, and undead** within 60 ft/12 tiles (not behind total cover) and know if **consecrated or desecrated ground** is within 60 ft/12 tiles. It is a **bonus action**. You have **one use** until your next long rest.

**Positioning:** From the treeline, Briarford's porch lanterns may be beyond 60 ft/12 tiles. Mordain must walk **halfway down the hill** (free narrative move) to bring the blue lantern and the forest path into range. If the player refuses to move closer, Divine Sense only reads the **path at his feet**.

| What Mordain senses | What it means at the table | DM truth |
|---------------------|----------------------------|----------|
| **Undead** presence toward the **blue porch lantern** | Something **unholy is tied to the light** — not necessarily the light itself being a god's sign. | **Bound souls** in the lantern network. |
| **No celestial** within range | The warmth in his chest is **not** a visible angel standing on the path. | Divine attention without manifest avatar. |
| **Desecrated ground** on the **downslope path** | The road into Briarford has been **walked wrong** — recent, repeated, intentional. | Necromantic influence / soul-led victims. |
| **No consecrated ground** within range | No active shrine blessing this approach. | Way-shrine fell long ago. |

If **Insight succeeded by 5+**, Mordain also feels his symbol **cool** when he faces the undead reading — guidance and warning **named** in sensation: *go, but go armed with truth.*

**Option 4 — Open descent**

- No test, no Divine Sense — walk down openly, holy symbol in hand, cloak thrown back to show mail.
- First villager on the path (**Perception DC 10** to spot early) sees a **half-orc in chain** and may run or freeze — roleplay only, no combat.
- **Advantage on Intimidation** if Mordain leans on presence; **disadvantage on Persuasion** with nervous locals unless he softens tone.
- Fastest path to tavern; villagers remember him.

**Clues to carry into Briarford (leave with at least two):**

- At least one porch lantern burns **blue** while the rest hold ordinary flame.
- **Contract notice** on the village hall board — Mayor Alden hires for unnatural lantern behavior.
- The pull **eases facing downhill**, **tightens** facing the forest.
- *(Optional)* Second lantern **out of sync** — matches Lilith's Perception / Elias's broadcast notes.
- *(Optional)* **Divine Sense** — undead tied to the blue light; **desecrated** approach path.
- *(Optional)* Way-shrine verse — *"Dead light sees the road"* — **Religion** hook toward seal lore (campaign part 2).
- *(Optional)* Hooded traveler on the downslope path — cross-thread with Vex or other road intros.

**Arriving in Briarford (all branches):** Mordain reaches the **village tavern** at dusk or full evening — hearth lit, a few locals, contract copied on the wall if the hall board is closed. Mayor Alden may be at the hall or sent for when a **half-orc in mail** asks about the blue lights. He introduces himself by name; the force that chose him stays **private** until he trusts the table. If **Elias**, **Lilith**, or **Harry Potter** arrive later, Mordain's story contrasts *"the light pulled me"* with their research and measurements.

### Contingencies

- **If Mordain skips all checks:** The pulse and contract notice are still two clues. Run the **Insight** complication anyway — that is the teach moment.
- **If Mordain uses Divine Sense from the treeline only:** He may get **desecrated ground** on the path beneath him but **not** the lantern undead yet. Offer the halfway descent without penalty.
- **If Mordain uses Lay on Hands on himself:** Allowed if he narrates anxiety as physical — flavour only; no damage taken in this scene.
- **If Mordain attacks the village or lantern:** No combat required. Locals bar doors; mayor refuses hire until calmed. Point toward **talk first** — session 1 convergence assumes cooperation.
- **If Insight fails and Divine Sense finds undead:** Cognitive dissonance is the point — he **felt certainty** and **sensed unholiness**. Roleplay doubt; do not resolve which wins until later sessions.
- **If the player references Lilith's herb ring or Elias's inn:** Optional — **Perception DC 15** at dusk might catch **silver-green herb-glow** upslope after midnight (Lilith's scene); at dusk, only normal treeline. Keep intros independent unless the table runs them same fictional evening.
- **If Mordain declares a deity or oath mid-scene:** Accept it — update flavor, not mechanics. The draft lore stays **unnamed power** until the player chooses.

### DM Notes

#### The force that chose him (DM only — share in pieces)

Months ago, on a road with no shrine in sight, Mordain felt **warmth settle behind his ribs** — not fever, not wound, not dream. No voice. No face. Only **direction**: east, then east again, then toward any rumour of light that burned wrong.

He has no temple story. No sworn oath. No name for the power except *the thing that will not leave*. Paladin mechanics apply — **Lay on Hands**, **Divine Sense** — but Mordain does not trust the source yet. Briarford's blue lantern is the first time the pull has aimed at a **place** he can see.

**What is actually true (campaign):** The lantern network is **bound souls** (undead) serving a necromancer. The pull Mordain feels is **real divine attention** — not the necromancer — but it **aligns** with the same broadcast because something holy wants witnesses at Briarford before the seal work completes. Do **not** explain this aloud in session 1. Let **Divine Sense** and later scenes carry the contradiction.

- **Spotlight rule:** Address the player directly; other PCs are not present. Solo screen time.
- **Teaching moment — Insight:** Reading **people and feelings**, including your own — failure is a **false read** (certainty), not bad luck. Same lesson as Vex's tavern scene, turned inward.
- **Teaching moment — Divine Sense:** **Bonus action**, **1/LR**, **60 ft/12 tiles**, celestials/fiends/**undead**, consecrated/desecrated ground. Let the player declare it; narrate sensations, not stat blocks. Undead ping on the blue lantern is the **hook** — do not explain bound souls yet.
- **Paladin without oath:** Mordain has class features but no sworn order. Divine Sense working **despite** doubt is intentional — mechanics prove something is real even when trust is not.
- **Cross-thread with Lilith:** Same hillside, different hour — Lilith **measures**; Mordain **feels**. Second out-of-sync lantern appears in both scenes.
- **Cross-thread with Elias:** Scholarly pattern vs divine pull — tavern line: *"Your notes match what my chest already knew."*
- **Cross-thread with Vex:** Hooded figure on downslope path; Mordain may enter tavern **before** Harry Potter arrives.
- **Timing:** Mordain can be **first at the tavern** (dusk) while other intros end at first light — hand-wave a meal and wait, or run his intro last so the party converges together.
- **Next scene:** Briarford tavern — Mayor Alden, other PCs, contract recognition.
